(This looks very similar also to&nbsp;\n     <a href=\"https://musingsaboutlibrarianship.blogspot.com/2019/12/more-science-mapping-tools-local.html\">Open Knowledge Graph's beta concept Map</a>&nbsp;except it doesn't have contribution data)\n    </div>\n    <div>\n     <br />\n    </div>\n    <div class=\"separator\" style=\"clear: both; So for example, if you want a comparison table comparing p-values or confidence interval you would need to use that predicate to the label the paper.\n    </div>\n    <div>\n     <br />\n    </div>\n    <div>\n     <a href=\"https://1.bp.blogspot.com/-Gl3uv5D5YAw/X2jemHC9alI/AAAAAAABYXo/fbi6n3yneuM0yyZHcEvpVvlHDGv7v3SiQCLcBGAsYHQ/s796/okrgrdata.png\" style=\"margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em; text-align: center;\"><img border=\"0\" data-original-height=\"765\" data-original-width=\"796\" height=\"385\" src=\"https://1.bp.blogspot.com/-Gl3uv5D5YAw/X2jemHC9alI/AAAAAAABYXo/fbi6n3yneuM0yyZHcEvpVvlHDGv7v3SiQCLcBGAsYHQ/w400-h385/okrgrdata.png\" width=\"400\" /></a>\n    </div>\n    <div>\n     <br />\n    </div>\n    <div>\n     <br />\n    </div>\n    <div>\n     So the success of this project depending on extracting sufficient human labour to enhance each paper with additional metadata , particularly on the contributions.\n    </div>\n    <div>\n     <br />\n    </div>\n    <div>\n     Unfortunately,&nbsp; when I tried to study the ontology and data model, I had problems wrapping my mind around how to add \"contributions\" for my papers. I have no doubt with some effort, I could figure it out, just as I more or less figured out Wikidata, but on first glance it looks complicated.&nbsp;\n    </div>\n    <div>\n     <br />\n    </div>\n    <div>\n     For example, I don't quite understand the idea and concept use of \"predicate templates\" and \"resources\". The Youtube video I found below didn't help either\n    </div>\n    <div>\n     <br />\n    </div>\n    <iframe allow=\"accelerometer; autoplay; clipboard-write; encrypted-media; gyroscope; picture-in-picture\" allowfullscreen=\"\" frameborder=\"0\" height=\"315\" src=\"https://www.youtube.com/embed/BhI-gngCl0k\" width=\"560\"></iframe>\n    <div>\n     <br />\n    </div>\n    <div>\n     However currently they are trying a&nbsp;\n     <a href=\"https://aoelen.github.io/\">ORKG Paper Annotator tool that might help users markup their paper with contributions etc.&nbsp;</a>&nbsp;I've given it a go and while it doesn't 100% solve the problem , it does seem promising.\n    </div>\n    <div>\n     <br />\n    </div>\n    <div class=\"separator\" style=\"clear: both; text-align: center;\">\n     <a href=\"https://1.bp.blogspot.com/-nRhw7PSFtTc/X53VJH_9R1I/AAAAAAABab4/KE1h-FZAnPsDU7k1AdONOQBTq0V8LTG2QCLcBGAsYHQ/s1338/orkg-smart.png\" style=\"margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;\"><img border=\"0\" data-original-height=\"612\" data-original-width=\"1338\" height=\"292\" src=\"https://1.bp.blogspot.com/-nRhw7PSFtTc/X53VJH_9R1I/AAAAAAABab4/KE1h-FZAnPsDU7k1AdONOQBTq0V8LTG2QCLcBGAsYHQ/w640-h292/orkg-smart.png\" width=\"640\" /></a>\n    </div>\n    <br />\n    <div style=\"text-align: center;\">\n     <a href=\"https://aoelen.github.io/\">ORKG Paper Annotator tool</a>\n    </div>\n    <div>\n     <br />\n    </div>\n    <div>\n     <br />\n    </div>\n    <h1>Ebsco Discovery Concept Map</h1>\n   </div>\n   <div>\n    So far, we have covered mostly open source applications of research graphs. But before these applications, one of the original uses of network maps and graph was the promise it would help with searching and discovery.\n   </div>\n   <div>\n    <br />\n   </div>\n   <div>\n    Currently I'm aware of two such commerical products,&nbsp;\n    <a href=\"https://www.yewno.com/discover\">the first is Yewno</a>, developed by a AI startup which has been in the market for a few years.&nbsp;\n   </div>\n   <div>\n    <br />\n   </div>\n   <div>\n    A more recent challenger in this space from a traditional library vendor is Ebsco's development of&nbsp;\n    <a href=\"https://connect.ebsco.com/s/article/Concept-Map-Quick-Start-Guide?language=en_US\">their Concept Map</a>&nbsp;feature which is currently in beta.\n   </div>\n   <div>\n    <br />\n   </div>\n   <iframe allow=\"accelerometer; autoplay; clipboard-write; encrypted-media; gyroscope; picture-in-picture\" allowfullscreen=\"\" frameborder=\"0\" height=\"315\" src=\"https://www.youtube.com/embed/pW7NO9BtJaU\" width=\"560\"></iframe>\n   <div>\n    <br />\n   </div>\n   <div>\n    The idea here is to map your normal keyword search terms to concepts and reated concepts that exist in Ebsco Discovery Service.\n   </div>\n   <div>\n    <br />\n   </div>\n   <div>\n    You can then build a boolean search by clicking on the concepts you want which adds them to the search builder.\n   </div>\n   <div>\n    <br />\n   </div>\n   <div class=\"separator\" style=\"clear: both;
