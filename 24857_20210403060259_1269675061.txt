It is based on the principle that a computer can autonomously improve its own performance on a given task by learning from data – sometimes even beyond the capabilities of humans.</p> \n<p>Today, machine learning can perform many advanced tasks, including but not limited to:</p> \n<ul>\n <li>Image Classification / Object Detection</li>\n <li>Audio Scene / Speech Recognition</li>\n <li>Forecasting (Eg. To shed some light on this, let’s first take a look at what <strong>edge</strong> in Edge AI really means.</p> \n<div style=\"height:16px\" aria-hidden=\"true\" class=\"wp-block-spacer\"></div> \n<h2><strong>Edge Computing vs Cloud Computing</strong></h2> \n<div style=\"height:16px\" aria-hidden=\"true\" class=\"wp-block-spacer\"></div> \n<p>In essence, both edge and cloud computing are meant to do the same things – process data, run algorithms, etc. However, the fundamental difference in edge and cloud computing is <strong>where the computing actually takes place</strong>.</p> \n<p>In <em>edge computing</em>, information processing occurs on distributed IoT devices that are on the field and in active deployment (or on the edge). Some examples of edge devices include smartphones, as well as various SBCs and microcontrollers.</p> \n<p>In <em>cloud computing</em>, however, that same information processing occurs at a centralised location, such as in a data centre.</p> \n<div style=\"height:15px\" aria-hidden=\"true\" class=\"wp-block-spacer\"></div> \n<div class=\"wp-block-image\">\n <figure class=\"aligncenter is-resized\">\n  <img src=\"https://lh5.googleusercontent.com/IqoplZlFfQuoaBalX-OuUCmEbbKwK0UVD4Aog2RUrrDiylu8-9momfjbI2raTOxM1P7SbWU11crZncduOy--PXW2XPcM-a3lO6YFA3dETZEhkcI0QMG9zgKcEZQIcDH_VV3Mx4Uk\" alt=\"\" width=\"500\" />\n  <figcaption>\n   <em>Source: California Technical Academy</em>\n  </figcaption>\n </figure>\n</div> \n<div style=\"height:15px\" aria-hidden=\"true\" class=\"wp-block-spacer\"></div> \n<p>Traditionally, cloud computing has dominated the IoT scene. Because it is powered by data centres that naturally have greater computing capabilities, IoT devices&nbsp; on the edge could simply transmit local data and maintain their key characteristics of low power consumption and affordability. Although cloud computing is still a very important and powerful tool for IoT, edge computing has been getting more attention lately for two important reasons.</p> \n<div style=\"height:10px\" aria-hidden=\"true\" class=\"wp-block-spacer\"></div> \n<p><strong>Reason 1: </strong>The hardware on edge devices has become more capable while remaining cost competitive.</p> \n<p><strong>Reason 2:</strong> Software is becoming increasingly optimised for edge devices.</p> \n<div style=\"height:10px\" aria-hidden=\"true\" class=\"wp-block-spacer\"></div> \n<p>This trend is progressing tremendously that it is now possible to run machine learning, which has long been ‘reserved’ for cloud computing due to high computational requirements, on edge computing devices! And thus, Edge AI was born.</p> \n<div style=\"height:15px\" aria-hidden=\"true\" class=\"wp-block-spacer\"></div> \n<h2><strong>Edge AI: Bringing Cloud to the Edge to Evolve IoT</strong></h2> \n<div style=\"height:15px\" aria-hidden=\"true\" class=\"wp-block-spacer\"></div> \n<p>With Edge AI, IoT devices are becoming smarter. What does that mean? Well, with machine learning, edge devices are now able to make decisions. They can <strong>make predictions</strong>, <strong>process complex data</strong>, and <strong>administer solutions</strong>.</p> \n<p>For example, edge IoT devices can process operating conditions to predict if a given piece of machinery will fail. This allows companies to perform predictive maintenance and avoid the larger damages and costs that would have been incurred in the event of a complete failure.</p> \n<p>On the other hand, a security camera equipped with edge AI may no longer only capture video. We will be able to identify humans and count foot traffic. Or, with facial recognition, even identify exactly who has passed through an area and when. But the crux of this paradigm shift is clear – more than ever, cloud capabilities are being moved to the edge; and for good reason.</p> \n<div style=\"height:15px\" aria-hidden=\"true\" class=\"wp-block-spacer\"></div> \n<hr class=\"wp-block-separator\" /> \n<div style=\"height:5px\" aria-hidden=\"true\" class=\"wp-block-spacer\"></div> \n<h2><strong>Benefits of Edge AI</strong></h2> \n<div style=\"height:15px\" aria-hidden=\"true\" class=\"wp-block-spacer\"></div> \n<h3><strong>1.&nbsp; Reduced Latency</strong></h3> \n<div style=\"height:10px\" aria-hidden=\"true\" class=\"wp-block-spacer\"></div> \n<p>The most direct advantage of processing information on the edge is that there is no longer a need to transmit data to and from the cloud. As a result, latencies in data processing can be greatly reduced.</p> \n<p>In the prior example of preventative maintenance, an Edge AI enabled device would be able to respond almost immediately to, for instance, shut the compromised machinery down. If we used cloud computing to perform the machine learning algorithm instead, we would lose at least a second of time during to the transmission of data to and from the cloud. While that may not sound significant, every margin of safety that can be achieved is often worth pursuing when it comes to operation-critical equipment!</p> \n<div style=\"height:10px\" aria-hidden=\"true\" class=\"wp-block-spacer\"></div> \n<h3><strong>2.&nbsp; Reduced Bandwidth Requirement and Cost</strong></h3> \n<div style=\"height:10px\" aria-hidden=\"true\" class=\"wp-block-spacer\"></div> \n<p>With less data being transmitted to and from edge IoT devices, there will be a lower requirement and thus costs in network bandwidth.</p> \n<p>Take an image classification task for example. With a reliance on cloud computing, the entire image must be sent for online processing. But if edge computing was used instead, there would no longer be a need to send that data. Instead, we can simply send the processed result, which is often several orders of magnitude smaller than the raw image. If we multiply this effect by the number of IoT devices in a network, which can be as many as thousands or more, these savings are nothing to scoff at!</p> \n<div style=\"height:10px\" aria-hidden=\"true\" class=\"wp-block-spacer\"></div> \n<h3><strong>3.&nbsp; Increased Data Security</strong></h3> \n<div style=\"height:10px\" aria-hidden=\"true\" class=\"wp-block-spacer\"></div> \n<p>A reduction in the transmission of data to external locations also means less open connections and fewer opportunities for cyber attacks. This keeps edge devices operating safely out of the reach of a potential intercept or data breach. Furthermore, since data is no longer stored in the centralised cloud, the consequences of a single breach are heavily mitigated.</p> \n<div style=\"height:10px\" aria-hidden=\"true\" class=\"wp-block-spacer\"></div> \n<h3><strong>4.&nbsp; Improved Reliability</strong></h3> \n<div style=\"height:10px\" aria-hidden=\"true\" class=\"wp-block-spacer\"></div> \n<p>With the distributed nature of Edge AI and edge computing, operational risks can also be distributed across the entire network. In essence, even if the centralised cloud computer or cluster fails, individual edge devices are able to maintain their functions since the computing processes are now independent from the cloud! This is especially important for critical IoT applications, such as in healthcare.</p> \n<div style=\"height:15px\" aria-hidden=\"true\" class=\"wp-block-spacer\"></div> \n<hr class=\"wp-block-separator\" /> \n<div style=\"height:5px\" aria-hidden=\"true\" class=\"wp-block-spacer\"></div> \n<h2><strong>Why is Edge AI Important?</strong></h2> \n<div style=\"height:15px\" aria-hidden=\"true\" class=\"wp-block-spacer\"></div> \n<p>While the tangible benefits of Edge AI are clear, its intrinsic impacts may be more elusive.</p> \n<div style=\"height:10px\" aria-hidden=\"true\" class=\"wp-block-spacer\"></div> \n<h3><strong>Edge AI Alters the Way we Live</strong></h3> \n<div style=\"height:10px\" aria-hidden=\"true\" class=\"wp-block-spacer\"></div> \n<p>For one, Edge AI represents the first wave of truly integrating artificial intelligence into daily living.
