Depending on your approach, you may ask for additional information at this point, including the make, model, and color of the customer’s vehicle or the number of the curbside pickup parking spot they’re parked in.&nbsp;</span></span></span></span></span></p> \n<p class=\"Body\" style=\"border:none\"><span style=\"color:#000000;\"><span style=\"font-size:12pt\"><span style=\"font-family:Calibri,sans-serif\"><span style=\"font-size:11.0pt\"><span style=\"font-family:&quot;Oracle Sans&quot;,sans-serif\">Here are some customer check-in options to consider:</span></span></span></span></span></p> \n<p class=\"Body\" style=\"border:none\"><span style=\"color:#000000;\"><span style=\"font-size:12pt\"><span style=\"font-family:Calibri,sans-serif\"><strong><i><span style=\"font-size:11.0pt\"><span style=\"font-family:&quot;Oracle Sans&quot;,sans-serif\">Check in by calling telephone number</span></span></i></strong><span style=\"font-size:11.0pt\"><span style=\"font-family:&quot;Oracle Sans&quot;,sans-serif\"><strong>.</strong> Whether this phone number is included in your curbside order ready notification or is listed on a sign near your designated curbside pickup parking spots, this is a low-tech way of handling check-ins. Many restaurants use this method. By speaking with the customer, it also gives you a chance to ask them any last-minute questions, such as whether they want plastic cutlery.</span></span></span></span></span></p> \n<p class=\"Body\" style=\"border:none\"><span style=\"color:#000000;\"><span style=\"font-size:12pt\"><span style=\"font-family:Calibri,sans-serif\"><strong><i><span style=\"font-size:11.0pt\"><span style=\"font-family:&quot;Oracle Sans&quot;,sans-serif\">Check in with staff stationed in the parking lot or at designated spot</span></span></i></strong><span style=\"font-size:11.0pt\"><span style=\"font-family:&quot;Oracle Sans&quot;,sans-serif\"><strong>.</strong> This approach takes the check-in burden off customers by having trained associates at the ready, often with handheld devices to check them in. Companies with an older customer base and those that need to ensure a fast curbside delivery process, such as those doing curbside along busy urban streets, may find this approach most appealing.</span></span></span></span></span></p> \n<p class=\"Body\" style=\"border:none\"><span style=\"color:#000000;\"><span style=\"font-size:12pt\"><span style=\"font-family:Calibri,sans-serif\"><span style=\"font-size:11.0pt\"><span style=\"font-family:&quot;Oracle Sans&quot;,sans-serif\">If you’re using either of these first two methods, then take advantage of the fact that your associates are talking to the customer before going out to their vehicle, says Kaiser. “Consider having the employee ask questions and provide friendly reminders,” he says. “For example, your store associate can tell the customer, ‘I will need to see your driver's license, but feel free to hold it up against your window.’ Or they can ask ahead of time where in the car the customer would like their items placed, such as the trunk or passenger side back seat. You might also tell them where you are putting the receipt.”</span></span></span></span></span></p> \n<p class=\"Body\" style=\"border:none\"><span style=\"color:#000000;\"><span style=\"font-size:12pt\"><span style=\"font-family:Calibri,sans-serif\"><strong><i><span style=\"font-size:11.0pt\"><span style=\"font-family:&quot;Oracle Sans&quot;,sans-serif\">Check in by clicking a link in the curbside order ready notification</span></span></i></strong><span style=\"font-size:11.0pt\"><span style=\"font-family:&quot;Oracle Sans&quot;,sans-serif\"><strong>.</strong> Whether the notification is delivered via SMS, email, or push, clicking a “check in” link in it and then completing the check-in form on the mobile website is a strong, scalable approach to handling check-ins, which are then routed to the store’s POS system.</span></span></span></span></span></p> \n<p class=\"Body\" style=\"border:none\"><span style=\"color:#000000;\"><span style=\"font-size:12pt\"><span style=\"font-family:Calibri,sans-serif\"><strong><i><span style=\"font-size:11.0pt\"><span style=\"font-family:&quot;Oracle Sans&quot;,sans-serif\">Check in by clicking button in mobile app</span></span></i></strong><span style=\"font-size:11.0pt\"><span style=\"font-family:&quot;Oracle Sans&quot;,sans-serif\"><strong>.</strong> This approach is even cleaner than checking in via a mobile website because the customer doesn’t have to complete a form. The mobile app already has all the details about the order and the customer, which allows the process to be streamlined</span></span></span></span></span></p> \n<p class=\"Body\" style=\"border:none\"><span style=\"color:#000000;\"><span style=\"font-size:12pt\"><span style=\"font-family:Calibri,sans-serif\"><strong><i><span style=\"font-size:11.0pt\"><span style=\"font-family:&quot;Oracle Sans&quot;,sans-serif\">Check in by replying to the</span></span></i></strong><span style=\"font-size:11.0pt\"><span style=\"font-family:&quot;Oracle Sans&quot;,sans-serif\"><strong> </strong><i><strong>curbside order ready notification.</strong> </i></span></span><span lang=\"DE\" style=\"font-size:11.0pt\"><span style=\"font-family:&quot;Oracle Sans&quot;,sans-serif\">This is </span></span><span style=\"font-size:11.0pt\"><span style=\"font-family:&quot;Oracle Sans&quot;,sans-serif\">when the curbside order ready notification is sent via SMS. This setup allows you to narrow the perimeter of the beacon to minimize false positives, where the customer happens to drive by the beacon area on their way somewhere else.</span></span></span></span></span></p> \n<p class=\"Body\" style=\"border:none\"><span style=\"color:#000000;\"><span style=\"font-size:12pt\"><span messages using at least email since it is the strongest channel of record.”</span></span></span></span></span></p> \n<p class=\"Body\" style=\"border:none\"><span style=\"color:#000000;\"><span
