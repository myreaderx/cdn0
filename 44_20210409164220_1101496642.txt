{"code":1,"isDone":false,"toInsertArticleList":[{"createdTime":"2021-04-10 00:41:42","updatedTime":"2021-04-10 00:41:42","title":"How to Extend the Range of a Garage Door","link":"https://www.wikihow.com/Extend-the-Range-of-a-Garage-Door","description":"<div class=\"mw-parser-output\">\n <p>It's frustrating to struggle with your garage door opener at the end of a long day, but luckily, there are a few simple tricks that might help you extend the range of your remote. The typical range of a garage door opener is about , but a number of things can affect how well it works. Improving the range of your opener might be as simple as changing out your batteries, although in some cases, you might need to relocate the transmitter to avoid interference from other devices. </p> \n <h2><span class=\"mw-editsection\"><span class=\"mw-editsection-bracket\">[</span><a target=\"_blank\" href=\"https://www.wikihow.com/index.php?title=Extend-the-Range-of-a-Garage-Door&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1\" title=\"Edit section: Steps\">Edit</a><span class=\"mw-editsection-bracket\">]</span></span><span class=\"mw-headline\" id=\"Steps\">Steps</span></h2> \n <h3><span class=\"mw-editsection\"><span class=\"mw-editsection-bracket\">[</span><a target=\"_blank\" href=\"https://www.wikihow.com/index.php?title=Extend-the-Range-of-a-Garage-Door&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2\" title=\"Edit section: Check whether the car visor interferes with the remote.\">Edit</a><span class=\"mw-editsection-bracket\">]</span></span><span class=\"mw-headline\" id=\"Check-whether-the-car-visor-interferes-with-the-remote.\">Check whether the car visor interferes with the remote.</span></h3> \n <ol>\n  <li>Hold the remote in your hand if it's clipped to your visor. Then, press the button to open your garage. If the opener works fine when you're holding it, but it doesn't work on the visor, there may be something in the visor interfering with the signal. In that case, extending the range on your opener might not make much of a difference—you just won't be able to use the remote from the visor.<sup id=\"_ref-1\" class=\"reference\" aria-label=\"Link to Reference 1\"><a target=\"_blank\" href=\"https://www.wikihow.com/Extend-the-Range-of-a-Garage-Door#_note-1\">[1]</a></sup>\n   <div class=\"mwimg  largeimage  floatcenter \" style=\"max-width:728px\">\n    <a target=\"_blank\" href=\"https://www.wikihow.com/Image:Extend-the-Range-of-a-Garage-Door-Step-1.jpg\" class=\"image\"><img alt=\"Extend the Range of a Garage Door Step 1.jpg\" src=\"https://www.wikihow.com/images/thumb/a/a5/Extend-the-Range-of-a-Garage-Door-Step-1.jpg/aid12493661-v4-728px-Extend-the-Range-of-a-Garage-Door-Step-1.jpg\" decoding=\"async\" width=\"728\" height=\"546\" class=\"whcdn\" /></a>\n   </div></li>\n </ol> \n <h3><span class=\"mw-editsection\"><span class=\"mw-editsection-bracket\">[</span><a target=\"_blank\" href=\"https://www.wikihow.com/index.php?title=Extend-the-Range-of-a-Garage-Door&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3\" title=\"Edit section: Replace the battery.\">Edit</a><span class=\"mw-editsection-bracket\">]</span></span><span class=\"mw-headline\" id=\"Replace-the-battery.\">Replace the battery.</span></h3> \n <ol>\n  <li>This simple fix only takes a few minutes. A failing battery can definitely impact how well your garage door opener works.<sup id=\"_ref-2\" class=\"reference\" aria-label=\"Link to Reference 2\"><a target=\"_blank\" href=\"https://www.wikihow.com/Extend-the-Range-of-a-Garage-Door#_note-2\">[2]</a></sup> To replace the battery in most remotes, remove the visor clip, then gently pry off the back cover with a flat-head screwdriver. Use the screwdriver to carefully pop out the old battery, then simply press the new battery into place and replace the cover.<sup id=\"_ref-3\" class=\"reference\" aria-label=\"Link to Reference 3\"><a target=\"_blank\" href=\"https://www.wikihow.com/Extend-the-Range-of-a-Garage-Door#_note-3\">[3]</a></sup>\n   <div class=\"mwimg  largeimage  floatcenter \" style=\"max-width:728px\">\n    <a target=\"_blank\" href=\"https://www.wikihow.com/Image:Extend-the-Range-of-a-Garage-Door-Step-2.jpg\" class=\"image\"><img alt=\"Extend the Range of a Garage Door Step 2.jpg\" src=\"https://www.wikihow.com/images/thumb/6/69/Extend-the-Range-of-a-Garage-Door-Step-2.jpg/aid12493661-v4-728px-Extend-the-Range-of-a-Garage-Door-Step-2.jpg\" decoding=\"async\" width=\"728\" height=\"546\" class=\"whcdn\" /></a>\n   </div> \n   <ul>\n    <li>Most garage door openers use a 2032 button battery, although some do use different sizes.</li> \n    <li>If you've recently changed the batteries but they still aren't working, test them with a battery tester. If the battery sat on the shelf for a long time before you bought it, it might have been dead when it got to you.</li>\n   </ul></li>\n </ol> \n <h3><span class=\"mw-editsection\"><span class=\"mw-editsection-bracket\">[</span><a target=\"_blank\" href=\"https://www.wikihow.com/index.php?title=Extend-the-Range-of-a-Garage-Door&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4\" title=\"Edit section: Extend your antenna.\">Edit</a><span class=\"mw-editsection-bracket\">]</span></span><span class=\"mw-headline\" id=\"Extend-your-antenna.\">Extend your antenna.</span></h3> \n <ol>\n  <li>Use this simple fix to boost your range. Find the antenna on your garage door opener—it's typically just an insulated wire hanging from the device. Turn off the power to your garage, then use wire strippers to cut off about of insulation from the end of the wire. Then, cut about of low-voltage copper wire and strip from one end of that, as well. Twist the two bare ends of wire together and wrap them in electrical cord, then stretch the new wire down the beam that supports your garage door when it's open. Secure the wire near your garage door.<sup id=\"_ref-4\" class=\"reference\" aria-label=\"Link to Reference 4\"><a target=\"_blank\" href=\"https://www.wikihow.com/Extend-the-Range-of-a-Garage-Door#_note-4\">[4]</a></sup>\n   <div class=\"mwimg  largeimage  floatcenter \" style=\"max-width:728px\">\n    <a target=\"_blank\" href=\"https://www.wikihow.com/Image:Extend-the-Range-of-a-Garage-Door-Step-3.jpg\" class=\"image\"><img alt=\"Extend the Range of a Garage Door Step 3.jpg\" src=\"https://www.wikihow.com/images/thumb/b/b2/Extend-the-Range-of-a-Garage-Door-Step-3.jpg/aid12493661-v4-728px-Extend-the-Range-of-a-Garage-Door-Step-3.jpg\" decoding=\"async\" width=\"728\" height=\"546\" class=\"whcdn\" /></a>\n   </div> \n   <ul>\n    <li>If you'd like, you can feed the new antenna over your garage door so it goes outside. This will boost your signal even more, but it's fine to leave it inside the door if you don't want to see the wire.<sup id=\"_ref-5\" class=\"reference\" aria-label=\"Link to Reference 5\"><a target=\"_blank\" href=\"https://www.wikihow.com/Extend-the-Range-of-a-Garage-Door#_note-5\">[5]</a></sup></li> \n    <li>You can use any wire you happen to have lying around, including stereo, ethernet, or telephone wire.<sup id=\"_ref-6\" class=\"reference\" aria-label=\"Link to Reference 6\"><a target=\"_blank\" href=\"https://www.wikihow.com/Extend-the-Range-of-a-Garage-Door#_note-6\">[6]</a></sup></li>\n   </ul></li>\n </ol> \n <h3><span class=\"mw-editsection\"><span class=\"mw-editsection-bracket\">[</span><a target=\"_blank\" href=\"https://www.wikihow.com/index.php?title=Extend-the-Range-of-a-Garage-Door&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5\" title=\"Edit section: Replace your remote if it's more than 10 years old.\">Edit</a><span class=\"mw-editsection-bracket\">]</span></span><span id=\"Replace-your-remote-if-it's-more-than-10-years-old.\"></span><span class=\"mw-headline\" id=\"Replace-your-remote-if-it.27s-more-than-10-years-old.\">Replace your remote if it's more than 10 years old.</span></h3> \n <ol>\n  <li>Try this if you think the contacts might be worn out. As the remote ages and the contacts wear down, the remote is no longer able to send a strong signal to the transmitter. Over time, this will affect the range of the garage door opener. You can purchase a replacement remote from most big-box stores that sell home improvement supplies.<sup id=\"_ref-7\" class=\"reference\" aria-label=\"Link to Reference 7\"><a target=\"_blank\" href=\"https://www.wikihow.com/Extend-the-Range-of-a-Garage-Door#_note-7\">[7]</a></sup>\n   <div class=\"mwimg  largeimage  floatcenter \" style=\"max-width:728px\">\n    <a target=\"_blank\" href=\"https://www.wikihow.com/Image:Extend-the-Range-of-a-Garage-Door-Step-4.jpg\" class=\"image\"><img alt=\"Extend the Range of a Garage Door Step 4.jpg\" src=\"https://www.wikihow.com/images/thumb/e/eb/Extend-the-Range-of-a-Garage-Door-Step-4.jpg/aid12493661-v4-728px-Extend-the-Range-of-a-Garage-Door-Step-4.jpg\" decoding=\"async\" width=\"728\" height=\"546\" class=\"whcdn\" /></a>\n   </div> \n   <ul>\n    <li>If the remote sat for a long time with old batteries, the circuits are likely corroded. In that case, the remote probably needs to be replaced even if it's less than 10 years old.<sup id=\"_ref-8\" class=\"reference\" aria-label=\"Link to Reference 8\"><a target=\"_blank\" href=\"https://www.wikihow.com/Extend-the-Range-of-a-Garage-Door#_note-8\">[8]</a></sup></li>\n   </ul></li>\n </ol> \n <h3><span class=\"mw-editsection\"><span class=\"mw-editsection-bracket\">[</span><a target=\"_blank\" href=\"https://www.wikihow.com/index.php?title=Extend-the-Range-of-a-Garage-Door&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6\" title=\"Edit section: Change the frequency of an older opener.\">Edit</a><span class=\"mw-editsection-bracket\">]</span></span><span class=\"mw-headline\" id=\"Change-the-frequency-of-an-older-opener.\">Change the frequency of an older opener.</span></h3> \n <ol>\n  <li>Flip the frequency switches if your remote has them. Older garage door remotes communicate with the transmitter on a certain frequency. This frequency is determined by a series of switches, known as DIP switches. Open the back of your remote and look for these switches—there might be 9 or 12, depending on the model of your opener. Then, look at your transmitter, or the box inside your garage that controls the opener, and find the corresponding switches. Alternate some of the switches from their current position—just make sure they match exactly on the remote and the transmitter.<sup id=\"_ref-9\" class=\"reference\" aria-label=\"Link to Reference 9\"><a target=\"_blank\" href=\"https://www.wikihow.com/Extend-the-Range-of-a-Garage-Door#_note-9\">[9]</a></sup>\n   <div class=\"mwimg  largeimage  floatcenter \" style=\"max-width:728px\">\n    <a target=\"_blank\" href=\"https://www.wikihow.com/Image:Extend-the-Range-of-a-Garage-Door-Step-5.jpg\" class=\"image\"><img alt=\"Extend the Range of a Garage Door Step 5.jpg\" src=\"https://www.wikihow.com/images/thumb/5/51/Extend-the-Range-of-a-Garage-Door-Step-5.jpg/aid12493661-v4-728px-Extend-the-Range-of-a-Garage-Door-Step-5.jpg\" decoding=\"async\" width=\"728\" height=\"546\" class=\"whcdn\" /></a>\n   </div> \n   <ul>\n    <li>It's better to use a random pattern for the switches, rather than putting them all in a straight row—that will make it less likely for someone to be able to copy your RF signal and open your garage door.</li> \n    <li>If your transmitter works on a frequency but it doesn't have switches, you can install an external receiver near the garage door opener that will convert the frequency for you.</li>\n   </ul></li>\n </ol> \n <h3><span class=\"mw-editsection\"><span class=\"mw-editsection-bracket\">[</span><a target=\"_blank\" href=\"https://www.wikihow.com/index.php?title=Extend-the-Range-of-a-Garage-Door&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7\" title=\"Edit section: Replace your lightbulbs if they're causing interference.\">Edit</a><span class=\"mw-editsection-bracket\">]</span></span><span id=\"Replace-your-lightbulbs-if-they're-causing-interference.\"></span><span class=\"mw-headline\" id=\"Replace-your-lightbulbs-if-they.27re-causing-interference.\">Replace your lightbulbs if they're causing interference.</span></h3> \n <ol>\n  <li>Unplug your garage lights to see if that's the problem. Some devices emit radio signals that can interfere with your garage door opener. LED and fluorescent lights are a frequent culprit, so if you have them installed in your garage, try unplugging them for a few minutes. If the range on your garage door opener improves when the lights are unplugged, try replacing the bulbs with a different brand.<sup id=\"_ref-10\" class=\"reference\" aria-label=\"Link to Reference 10\"><a target=\"_blank\" href=\"https://www.wikihow.com/Extend-the-Range-of-a-Garage-Door#_note-10\">[10]</a></sup>\n   <div class=\"mwimg  largeimage  floatcenter \" style=\"max-width:728px\">\n    <a target=\"_blank\" href=\"https://www.wikihow.com/Image:Extend-the-Range-of-a-Garage-Door-Step-6.jpg\" class=\"image\"><img alt=\"Extend the Range of a Garage Door Step 6.jpg\" src=\"https://www.wikihow.com/images/thumb/7/76/Extend-the-Range-of-a-Garage-Door-Step-6.jpg/aid12493661-v4-728px-Extend-the-Range-of-a-Garage-Door-Step-6.jpg\" decoding=\"async\" width=\"728\" height=\"546\" class=\"whcdn\" /></a>\n   </div> \n   <ul>\n    <li>If you don't want to replace your lightbulbs, it may help to install a ferrite clip around the power cord on the box that controls your garage door opener. Simply clamp the clip around the power cord and snap it into place to help block out RF interference. You can order these cores online or purchase them from an electronic store.</li>\n   </ul></li>\n </ol> \n <h3><span class=\"mw-editsection\"><span class=\"mw-editsection-bracket\">[</span><a target=\"_blank\" href=\"https://www.wikihow.com/index.php?title=Extend-the-Range-of-a-Garage-Door&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8\" title=\"Edit section: Move the opener control box if you can't remove the interference.\">Edit</a><span class=\"mw-editsection-bracket\">]</span></span><span id=\"Move-the-opener-control-box-if-you-can't-remove-the-interference.\"></span><span class=\"mw-headline\" id=\"Move-the-opener-control-box-if-you-can.27t-remove-the-interference.\">Move the opener control box if you can't remove the interference.</span></h3> \n <ol>\n  <li>Place it further away from other electrical devices. There are a number of devices that could cause interference, including automated sprinkler systems or lights, surge protectors, and alarms. If you're able to identify what's causing the interference and it's not an option to move it, moving the box that controls your garage door opener may help solve the problem. Just keep in mind that you may need to have a professional do the installation for you.<sup id=\"_ref-11\" class=\"reference\" aria-label=\"Link to Reference 11\"><a target=\"_blank\" href=\"https://www.wikihow.com/Extend-the-Range-of-a-Garage-Door#_note-11\">[11]</a></sup>\n   <div class=\"mwimg  largeimage  floatcenter \" style=\"max-width:728px\">\n    <a target=\"_blank\" href=\"https://www.wikihow.com/Image:Extend-the-Range-of-a-Garage-Door-Step-7.jpg\" class=\"image\"><img alt=\"Extend the Range of a Garage Door Step 7.jpg\" src=\"https://www.wikihow.com/images/thumb/f/fe/Extend-the-Range-of-a-Garage-Door-Step-7.jpg/aid12493661-v4-728px-Extend-the-Range-of-a-Garage-Door-Step-7.jpg\" decoding=\"async\" width=\"728\" height=\"546\" class=\"whcdn\" /></a>\n   </div> \n   <ul>\n    <li>For instance, if you have a cable running along the back of your garage that's causing interference, re-installing your garage door opener box toward the front of your garage may help.</li> \n    <li>Unfortunately, it can be hard to pinpoint what's exactly causing the problem—it could be something like a nearby airport or radio tower, for instance.<sup id=\"_ref-12\" class=\"reference\" aria-label=\"Link to Reference 12\"><a target=\"_blank\" href=\"https://www.wikihow.com/Extend-the-Range-of-a-Garage-Door#_note-12\">[12]</a></sup></li>\n   </ul></li>\n </ol> \n <h3><span class=\"mw-editsection\"><span class=\"mw-editsection-bracket\">[</span><a target=\"_blank\" href=\"https://www.wikihow.com/index.php?title=Extend-the-Range-of-a-Garage-Door&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9\" title=\"Edit section: Reprogram the remote if it uses a code.\">Edit</a><span class=\"mw-editsection-bracket\">]</span></span><span class=\"mw-headline\" id=\"Reprogram-the-remote-if-it-uses-a-code.\">Reprogram the remote if it uses a code.</span></h3> \n <ol>\n  <li>Reset the remote if it's no longer responding at all. If your garage door opener isn't working, something may have happened to reset the code it transmits to the receiver.<sup id=\"_ref-13\" class=\"reference\" aria-label=\"Link to Reference 13\"><a target=\"_blank\" href=\"https://www.wikihow.com/Extend-the-Range-of-a-Garage-Door#_note-13\">[13]</a></sup> While the process may vary depending on your garage door opener, most major brands have a button on the opener or the wall control that says \"Learn.\" Press this button, then press and hold the button on your opener that you want to use to open the door.<sup id=\"_ref-14\" decoding=\"async\" width=\"728\" height=\"546\" class=\"whcdn\" /></a>\n   </div> \n   <ul>\n    <li>If the button is on the box that controls your garage door opener, it will be near the base of the antenna.</li> \n    <li>Depending on your model, you may hear a click on the garage door, or you might see flashing lights on the remote that indicate the programming worked.</li> \n    <li>When Since wavy hair tends to be a little more frizzy and dry than straight hair, it’s important to use gentle, hydrating products.<sup id=\"_ref-1\" class=\"reference\" aria-label=\"Link to Reference 1\"><a target=\"_blank\" href=\"https://www.wikihow.com/Accentuate-Wavy-Hair#_note-1\">[1]</a></sup><br /><br />\n   <div class=\"mwimg  largeimage  floatcenter \" style=\"max-width:728px\">\n    <a target=\"_blank\" href=\"https://www.wikihow.com/Image:Accentuate-Wavy-Hair-Step-1-Version-3.jpg\" class=\"image\"><img alt=\"Accentuate Wavy Hair Step 1 Version 3.jpg\" src=\"https://www.wikihow.com/images/thumb/e/e6/Accentuate-Wavy-Hair-Step-1-Version-3.jpg/aid1432084-v4-728px-Accentuate-Wavy-Hair-Step-1-Version-3.jpg\" decoding=\"async\" width=\"728\" height=\"410\" class=\"whcdn\" /></a>\n   </div> \n   <ul>\n    <li>Look for shampoo that says “moisturizing” or “hydrating” on the bottle.</li> \n    <li>It’s up to you how often you wash your hair, but try not to wash it every single day. Using shampoo too often can strip your hair of its natural oils, leaving it frizzy.</li>\n   </ul></li>\n </ol> \n <h3><span class=\"mw-editsection\"><span class=\"mw-editsection-bracket\">[</span><a target=\"_blank\" href=\"https://www.wikihow.com/index.php?title=Accentuate-Wavy-Hair&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3\" title=\"Edit section: Condition your hair to fight frizz.\">Edit</a><span class=\"mw-editsection-bracket\">]</span></span><span class=\"mw-headline\" id=\"Condition-your-hair-to-fight-frizz.\">Condition your hair to fight frizz.</span></h3> \n <ol>\n  <li>Every time you wash your hair, use a medium consistency conditioner. Heavy, deep conditioning products can weigh your hair down, so try to find products that are designed for wavy hair to fight frizz and help your waves form.<sup id=\"_ref-2\" class=\"reference\" aria-label=\"Link to Reference 2\"><a target=\"_blank\" href=\"https://www.wikihow.com/Accentuate-Wavy-Hair#_note-2\">[2]</a></sup><br /><br /> \n   <ul>\n    <li>Stay away from silicone and sulfate products as you look for a conditioner. These ingredients can actually make your hair drier, leading to more frizz.</li>\n   </ul></li>\n </ol> \n <h3><span class=\"mw-editsection\"><span class=\"mw-editsection-bracket\">[</span><a target=\"_blank\" href=\"https://www.wikihow.com/index.php?title=Accentuate-Wavy-Hair&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4\" title=\"Edit section: Use cold water to rinse your hair.\">Edit</a><span class=\"mw-editsection-bracket\">]</span></span><span class=\"mw-headline\" id=\"Use-cold-water-to-rinse-your-hair.\">Use cold water to rinse your hair.</span></h3> \n <ol>\n  <li>Wash with warm, but rinse with cold water. The cold water will close your hair cuticles, leading to a smoother, shinier finish as your hair dries.<sup id=\"_ref-3\" class=\"reference\" aria-label=\"Link to Reference 3\"><a target=\"_blank\" href=\"https://www.wikihow.com/Accentuate-Wavy-Hair#_note-3\">[3]</a></sup><br /><br />\n   <div class=\"mwimg  largeimage  floatcenter \" style=\"max-width:728px\">\n    <a target=\"_blank\" href=\"https://www.wikihow.com/Image:Accentuate-Wavy-Hair-Step-3-Version-3.jpg\" class=\"image\"><img alt=\"Accentuate Wavy Hair Step 3 Version 3.jpg\" src=\"https://www.wikihow.com/images/thumb/8/87/Accentuate-Wavy-Hair-Step-3-Version-3.jpg/aid1432084-v4-728px-Accentuate-Wavy-Hair-Step-3-Version-3.jpg\" decoding=\"async\" width=\"728\" height=\"410\" class=\"whcdn\" /></a>\n   </div> \n   <ul>\n    <li>It can be tough to get used to a cold water rinse, but it may actually help wake you up in the mornings!</li>\n   </ul></li>\n </ol> \n <h3><span class=\"mw-editsection\"><span class=\"mw-editsection-bracket\">[</span><a target=\"_blank\" href=\"https://www.wikihow.com/index.php?title=Accentuate-Wavy-Hair&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5\" title=\"Edit section: Pat your hair dry with a towel.\">Edit</a><span class=\"mw-editsection-bracket\">]</span></span><span class=\"mw-headline\" id=\"Pat-your-hair-dry-with-a-towel.\">Pat your hair dry with a towel.</span></h3> \n <ol>\n  <li>Use a microfiber towel to avoid frizz and soak up extra moisture. Flip your head upside down and soak up any excess water by patting your hair. Don’t be tempted to rub hair with the towel, because this will make your hair frizzy. You can also wrap your hair on top of your head with a dry towel for a few minutes if you need to.<sup id=\"_ref-4\" class=\"reference\" aria-label=\"Link to Reference 4\"><a target=\"_blank\" href=\"https://www.wikihow.com/Accentuate-Wavy-Hair#_note-4\">[4]</a></sup><br /><br /> \n   <ul>\n    <li>Your hair will still be damp, but not soaking wet, after towel drying.</li>\n   </ul></li>\n </ol> \n <h3><span class=\"mw-editsection\"><span class=\"mw-editsection-bracket\">[</span><a target=\"_blank\" href=\"https://www.wikihow.com/index.php?title=Accentuate-Wavy-Hair&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6\" title=\"Edit section: Comb your hair with a wide-toothed comb.\">Edit</a><span class=\"mw-editsection-bracket\">]</span></span><span class=\"mw-headline\" id=\"Comb-your-hair-with-a-wide-toothed-comb.\">Comb your hair with a wide-toothed comb.</span></h3> \n <ol>\n  <li>Brushing your hair with a hairbrush can lead to extra frizz. This is the backbone behind positive parenting techniques, for example, which argue that you reinforce good behavior by noticing it and appreciating it.<sup id=\"_ref-1\" class=\"reference\" aria-label=\"Link to Reference 1\"><a target=\"_blank\" href=\"https://www.wikihow.com/Say-Nice-Things#_note-1\">[1]</a></sup>\n   <div class=\"mwimg  largeimage  floatcenter \" style=\"max-width:728px\">\n    <a target=\"_blank\" href=\"https://www.wikihow.com/Image:Say-Nice-Things-Step-1.jpg\" class=\"image\"><img alt=\"Say Nice Things Step 1.jpg\" src=\"https://www.wikihow.com/images/thumb/e/e5/Say-Nice-Things-Step-1.jpg/aid12484295-v4-728px-Say-Nice-Things-Step-1.jpg\" decoding=\"async\" width=\"728\" height=\"546\" class=\"whcdn\" /></a>\n   </div> \n   <ul>\n    <li>For example, instead of waiting for someone to make a mistake before you say something, look for something good. Make the nice thought meaningful by tailoring it to the person you're praising. It's easy to focus on compliments for specific actions, but don't forget that you can just say thank you. <p>Replacing your ceiling drywall will take a little bit of time and effort, but it isn’t difficult if you have the right tools and materials. Clear the room, walls, and attic, if there is one above the ceiling. Pull out the insulation and set it aside so you can replace it later. Then, remove any fixtures and take down the old ceiling drywall. Use drywall adhesive to hold the new ceiling drywall up so you can fasten it with screws. The job will be difficult to do on your own, so consider renting a drywall lift or asking a friend to help you. </p> \n <h2><span class=\"mw-editsection\"><span class=\"mw-editsection-bracket\">[</span><a target=\"_blank\" href=\"https://www.wikihow.com/index.php?title=Replace-Ceiling-Drywall&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1\" title=\"Edit section: Steps\">Edit</a><span class=\"mw-editsection-bracket\">]</span></span><span class=\"mw-headline\" id=\"Steps\">Steps</span></h2> \n <h3><span class=\"mw-editsection\"><span class=\"mw-editsection-bracket\">[</span><a target=\"_blank\" href=\"https://www.wikihow.com/index.php?title=Replace-Ceiling-Drywall&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2\" title=\"Edit section: Clearing the Area and Removing Insulation\">Edit</a><span class=\"mw-editsection-bracket\">]</span></span><span class=\"mw-headline\" id=\"Clearing-the-Area-and-Removing-Insulation\">Clearing the Area and Removing Insulation</span></h3> \n <ol>\n  <li>Put on gloves, safety glasses, and a face mask. Ceilings, attics, and drywall can be dusty, dirty, and contain fiberglass and other particles that can get into your skin and eyes and irritate your throat and lungs if you breathe it in. Before you start working, put on some thick work gloves, safety glasses, and a face mask so you’re protected.<sup id=\"_ref-1\" class=\"reference\" aria-label=\"Link to Reference 1\"><a target=\"_blank\" href=\"https://www.wikihow.com/Replace-Ceiling-Drywall#_note-1\">[1]</a></sup>\n   <div class=\"mwimg  largeimage  floatcenter \" style=\"max-width:728px\">\n    <a target=\"_blank\" href=\"https://www.wikihow.com/Image:Replace-Ceiling-Drywall-Step-1.jpg\" class=\"image\"><img alt=\"Replace Ceiling Drywall Step 1.jpg\" src=\"https://www.wikihow.com/images/thumb/3/3f/Replace-Ceiling-Drywall-Step-1.jpg/aid11511417-v4-728px-Replace-Ceiling-Drywall-Step-1.jpg\" decoding=\"async\" width=\"728\" height=\"546\" class=\"whcdn\" /></a>\n   </div> \n   <ul>\n    <li>You can find safety equipment at home improvement stores, at department stores, and online.</li> \n    <li>Make sure the glasses fit snugly over your eyes so dust can’t get in on the sides.</li> \n    <li>You may want to wear a thick pair of jeans and a long-sleeved shirt to add additional protection to your skin.</li>\n   </ul></li> \n  <li>Take out any items stored in the attic, if necessary. Remove any picture frames and decor from the walls and set them in another room so they’re out of the way.
