href=\"https://www.mark-harvey.com/\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noopener noreferrer\">Mark Harvey</a>, who is known for his striking equine and canine photography, shifted his focus to the avian creatures gliding above his home in the Norfolk Broads. Now part of a series titled <em><a href=\"https://shop.mark-harvey.com/\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noopener noreferrer\">In Flight</a>,</em> the exquisitely detailed shots frame common birds —including magpies, blue tits, starlings, goldfinches, great tits, coal tits, long-tailed tits, and green finches—in otherwise unseen poses: some splay out an entire wingspan, while others wrap their feathers around the front of their torsos.</p>\n<p>Hearkening back to the methods of famed birdwatcher <a href=\"https://www.hasselblad.com/history/victor-hasselblad/\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noopener noreferrer\">Victor Hasselblad</a>, Harvey employed similar techniques to capture the dramatic shots. He used a slow, medium format with the same camera Hasselblad manufactured for the outdoor endeavor, taking just one image at a time.</p>\n<p>Harvey just released limited edition prints of the <em>In Flight</em> series, which are available in a run of 15 per subject in <a style=\"font-size: 1rem;\" href=\"https://shop.mark-harvey.com/\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noopener noreferrer\">his shop</a>, and shares more of his striking horse and pup portraits on <a href=\"https://www.instagram.com/markharveyphoto/\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noopener noreferrer\">Instagram</a>.
