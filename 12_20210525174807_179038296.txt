<em>Each/Other: Marie Watt and Cannupa Hanska Luger, </em>which opens this weekend at the <a href=\"https://www.denverartmuseum.org/en\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noopener\">Denver Art Museum</a>. The expansive exhibition—featuring 26 mixed-media sculptures, installations, and wall hangings—joins two of the leading Indigenous artists working today in a manner that distinguishes both the connective threads and nuances within their bodies of work.</p>\n<p>Situated at the center of the space is the 16-foot creature the pair created together by fashioning about 700 patterned bandannas submitted by an international crew around a steel armature. The collaborative installation, titled &#8220;Each/Other,&#8221; physically tethers Watt&#8217;s and Luger&#8217;s individual artworks while drawing on the socially engaged aspects inherent to both of their practices.</p>\n<p>&#160;</p>\n<div id=\"attachment_145092\" style=\"width: 2010px\" class=\"wp-caption alignnone\"><img aria-describedby=\"caption-attachment-145092\" loading=\"lazy\" class=\"wp-image-145092 size-full\" src=\"https://www.thisiscolossal.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/05/eachother-2.jpg\" alt=\"\" width=\"2000\" height=\"2065\" srcset=\"https://www.thisiscolossal.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/05/eachother-2.jpg 2000w, https://www.thisiscolossal.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/05/eachother-2-640x661.jpg 640w, https://www.thisiscolossal.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/05/eachother-2-960x991.jpg 960w, https://www.thisiscolossal.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/05/eachother-2-1488x1536.jpg 1488w, https://www.thisiscolossal.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/05/eachother-2-1984x2048.jpg 1984w, https://www.thisiscolossal.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/05/eachother-2-624x644.jpg 624w, https://www.thisiscolossal.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/05/eachother-2-640x661@2x.jpg 1280w, https://www.thisiscolossal.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/05/eachother-2-960x991@2x.jpg 1920w\" sizes=\"(max-width: 2000px) 100vw, 2000px\" /><p id=\"caption-attachment-145092\" class=\"wp-caption-text\">Cannupa Hanska Luger, &#8220;Every One&#8221; (2018), ceramic, social collaboration, 12 x 15 x 3 feet. For example, his 2018 piece &#8220;Every One&#8221; strings together 4,096 ceramic beads into a pixelated portrait of a young figure. Each individual orb represents one of the women, girls, and queer and trans folks who have been murdered or gone missing in Canada.</p>\n<p><a href=\"https://www.mariewattstudio.com/\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noopener\">Watt</a>, who is a member of the Seneca Nation and has Scottish and German heritage, utilizes everyday objects steeped in historical narratives and collective memory. British artist <a href=\"http://www.mattsimmonds.com/index.html\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noopener\">Matthew Simmonds</a> (<a href=\"https://www.thisiscolossal.com/?s=Matthew+Simmonds\">previously</a>) fuses these two traditional forms and honors their history with his miniature models carved into hunks of the raw stone. Evoking ancient ruins and sacred architecture—most pieces aren&#8217;t modeled after specific structures—the chiseled sculptures are complete with grand archways, ornately tiled ceilings, and minuscule statues on display in their halls.</p>\n<p>Within the spaces, Simmonds contrasts the rough, jagged edges of the stone with precise angles and detailed flourishes. &#8220;Drawing on the formal language and philosophy of architecture the work explores themes of positive and negative form, the significance of light and darkness, and the relationship between nature and human endeavor,&#8221;
