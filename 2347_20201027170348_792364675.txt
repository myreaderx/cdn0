And while the outcome of the Justice Department’s suit—which could take years to play out—is far from clear, analysts and investors say losing that deal could be a sizable blow to Apple, given estimates that Google’s payments account for up to a fifth of the iPhone maker’s overall profit.</span></p><p style=\"text-align: left;\"><br></p><p style=\"text-align: left;\"><span style=\"color: rgb(62, 62, 62);font-family: Georgia, serif;font-size: 16px;letter-spacing: 0.54px;text-align: left;background-color: rgb(255, 255, 255);\">“There’s a risk, if you play it out, that there actually could be more financial impact to Apple than there is for Google,” said Toni Sacconaghi, an analyst for Bernstein. He estimates that Apple’s stock could fall as much as 20% if the deal with Google were to be eliminated entirely. At the same time, he and others say, any damage could be far less if Apple is able to offset it through other deals involving Google and its competitors, as many investors and analysts say could happen.</span></p><p style=\"text-align: left;\"><br></p><p style=\"text-align: left;\"><span style=\"color: rgb(62, 62, 62);font-family: Georgia, serif;font-size: 16px;letter-spacing: 0.54px;text-align: left;background-color: rgb(255, 255, 255);\">Investors seemed to shrug off the threat last week when the Justice Department’s lawsuit against Google was revealed. Information about large payments from Google to Apple emerged in 2016 during an unrelated court fight involving the search giant, during which it was mentioned in court proceedings that Apple received $1 billion in 2014 as part of the arrangement.</span></p><p style=\"text-align: left;\"><br></p><p style=\"text-align: left;\"><span style=\"color: rgb(62, 62, 62);font-family: Georgia, serif;font-size: 16px;letter-spacing: 0.54px;text-align: left;background-color: rgb(255, 255, 255);\">The number grew sharply after that, analysts say, though they vary on its exact size.
