Since day one, you could use our choropleth maps to create <em>continuous</em> color scales – but using <em>stepped</em> color scales required a workaround.</p> \n<p>Not anymore. Creating stepped color scales is now easier than ever. To learn more about the new feature, check out our Academy article <a href=\"https://academy.datawrapper.de/article/294-how-to-customize-stepped-color-scales\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noopener\">“How to customize stepped color scales”</a>.</p> \n<p><img src=\"https://blog.datawrapper.de/img/200929_new-maps1.gif\" alt=\"GIF of the Datawrapper interface, and how a user switches between continuous and stepped color scales. Also new: You can rename and reorder items of categorical color legends directly in the Visualize step.</p> \n<p><img src=\"https://blog.datawrapper.de/img/full-200929_new-maps7.png\" alt=\"examples of the new Datawrapper color legends: categories and 3 x vertical continuous scales\" class=\"b-lazy\" data-src=\"/img/full-200929_new-maps7.png\" data-src-thumb=\"/img/thumb-200929_new-maps7.png\"><em>Examples of color legends now possible with Datawrapper. All legends can also be rotated to a horizontal position.</em></img></p> \n<p>And you can place your legend freely: If it overlaps important regions, move it above or below the map. Or choose an empty corner of your map and use our new offset and padding feature to find a good spot:</p> \n<img src=\"https://img.datawrapper.de/iUHaD/full.png\"><p>Another feature you’ll probably enjoy: If you (or your reader) hover over these nice-looking legends, you can now see the corresponding areas on the map –&nbsp;and the other way around. Just <strong>“Enable highlighting on hover”</strong>. Try it out on the map above.</p><p><img src=\"https://blog.datawrapper.de/img/200929_new-maps2.gif\" alt=\"GIF of the new &quot;Enable highlighting on hover&quot; feature.
