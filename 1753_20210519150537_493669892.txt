There are a number of subgenres of metal as well: <strong>Thrash Metal</strong> (Slayer, Megadeath, Metallica), <strong>Metalcore</strong> (Architects, While She Sleeps, August Burns Red), <strong>Deathmetal</strong> (Autopsy, Morbid Angel, Obituary), <strong>Alternate Metal</strong> (System of a Down, Killswitch Engage, Tool), and even <a href=\"https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WIKqgE4BwAY&amp;ab_channel=BABYMETAL\">Baby Metal</a>! Those are just a few, but listening to any metal music, you'll easily understand how that translated into the look and feel you see today. Keep this in the back of your head as you design your logotype because the subgenres of metal can be rather diverse.</p>\n<h3>Step 1</h3>\n<p>It will be extremely beneficial to view existing logotypes in this space for a better idea of the look and feel we're trying to achieve. This <a href=\"https://bmldb.com/\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noopener\">handy dandy website</a> is a perfect place to explore.</p>\n<figure class=\"post_image\"><img src=\"https://cms-assets.tutsplus.com/cdn-cgi/image/width=850/uploads/users/426/posts/21875/image-upload/logo-examples.jpg\" alt=\"Metal Band Logos\" loading=\"lazy\" width=\"870px\" height=\"1093px\" class=\"resized-image resized-image-desktop\" srcset=\"https://cms-assets.tutsplus.com/cdn-cgi/image/width=1700/uploads/users/426/posts/21875/image-upload/logo-examples.jpg 2x\"><img src=\"https://cms-assets.tutsplus.com/cdn-cgi/image/width=630/uploads/users/426/posts/21875/image-upload/logo-examples.jpg\" alt=\"Metal Band Logos\" loading=\"lazy\" width=\"650px\" height=\"815px\" class=\"resized-image resized-image-tablet\" srcset=\"https://cms-assets.tutsplus.com/cdn-cgi/image/width=1260/uploads/users/426/posts/21875/image-upload/logo-examples.jpg 2x\"><img src=\"https://cms-assets.tutsplus.com/cdn-cgi/image/width=360/uploads/users/426/posts/21875/image-upload/logo-examples.jpg\" alt=\"Metal Band Logos\" loading=\"lazy\" width=\"380px\" height=\"474px\" class=\"resized-image resized-image-mobile\" srcset=\"https://cms-assets.tutsplus.com/cdn-cgi/image/width=720/uploads/users/426/posts/21875/image-upload/logo-examples.jpg 2x\"></figure>\n<h3>Step 2</h3>\n<p>First and foremost, the metal band logo expert Christophe Szpajdel has key advice to break down the basics of a metal logo in a <a href=\"https://www.wired.com/2015/10/the-beauty-and-total-illegibility-of-extreme-metal-logos/\" target=\"_self\">WIRED interview</a>:</p>\n<blockquote>\n<p><span>I think the lettering should be sharp, inspired by gothic/old English fonts. First and last letters should be bigger than the middle ones. Unlike most people who think a black metal logo should contain symbols like pentagrams, inverted crosses... I think this is overdone.</span></p>\n</blockquote>\n<h2>\n<span class=\"sectionnum\">3.</span> Sketch a Custom Metal Logotype </h2>\n<h3>Step 1</h3>\n<p>As we talked about previously, we first need to understand the client and what kind of metal should influence the overall look and feel. For this particular project, I'll be drawing a custom logotype for me, myself and I. Additionally, being a huge fan of the \"Metalcore\" genre, I am going to explore that idea with this particular logotype. Metalcore bands oddly enough tend to be a bit on the \"cleaner\" side, so we'll keep that in mind going forward. Let's get to work!</p>\n<figure class=\"post_image\"><img src=\"https://cms-assets.tutsplus.com/cdn-cgi/image/width=850/uploads/users/426/posts/21875/image-upload/Metal-logotype-sketches.jpg\" alt=\"Logotype Concepts\" loading=\"lazy\" width=\"870px\" height=\"657px\" class=\"resized-image resized-image-desktop\" srcset=\"https://cms-assets.tutsplus.com/cdn-cgi/image/width=1700/uploads/users/426/posts/21875/image-upload/Metal-logotype-sketches.jpg 2x\"><img src=\"https://cms-assets.tutsplus.com/cdn-cgi/image/width=630/uploads/users/426/posts/21875/image-upload/Metal-logotype-sketches.jpg\" alt=\"Logotype Concepts\" loading=\"lazy\" width=\"650px\" height=\"492px\" class=\"resized-image resized-image-tablet\" srcset=\"https://cms-assets.tutsplus.com/cdn-cgi/image/width=1260/uploads/users/426/posts/21875/image-upload/Metal-logotype-sketches.jpg 2x\"><img src=\"https://cms-assets.tutsplus.com/cdn-cgi/image/width=360/uploads/users/426/posts/21875/image-upload/Metal-logotype-sketches.jpg\" alt=\"Logotype Concepts\" loading=\"lazy\" width=\"380px\" height=\"290px\" class=\"resized-image resized-image-mobile\" srcset=\"https://cms-assets.tutsplus.com/cdn-cgi/image/width=720/uploads/users/426/posts/21875/image-upload/Metal-logotype-sketches.jpg 2x\"></figure>\n<h3>Step 2 </h3>\n<p>Since metal logotypes are incredibly detailed and complex works of art, let's try and break it down to the bare minimum first. I'll be using Procreate for this entire process, but feel free to use Photoshop or even do things the analog way with pencil and paper!</p>\n<p>Begin by exploring the overall color (or visual weight) that your letterforms have. Is it super wide and bold? Maybe thin and wispy? Somewhere in between? Does the logotype have multiple words? That's something to consider as you go deeper into this process. Is it not looking how you've envisioned it? That's perfectly fine! This is a building up process, so be patient with yourself as you proceed.</p>\n<p>Through our research, we can easily derive that one of the key elements of a metal logo is the symmetrical shape it takes on. Now, the logotype doesn't necessarily have to be symmetrical—you could attempt something similar to Black Sabbath or Slayer, with angular, cleaner forms. However, for my logotype, I'll be exploring the symmetry.</p>\n<h3>Step 3</h3>\n<p>Consider additional illustrative elements. Does the logo have an upside-down cross? Maybe a sword or an axe? How can we show something dark, evil, or heavy through the use of illustration?
