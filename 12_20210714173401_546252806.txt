In her ongoing series <em><a href=\"https://juliehrudova.com/Herons-of-Amsterdam\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noopener\">Herons of Amsterdam</a></em>, photographer <a href=\"https://juliehrudova.com/\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noopener\">Julie Hrudová</a> documents the thriving feathered population—it&#8217;s grown considerably in recent decades, and in 2017, officials estimated there were <a href=\"https://www.theguardian.com/cities/gallery/2017/jun/05/urban-herons-amsterdam-in-pictures\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noopener\">800 pairs</a> living in 25 neighborhoods—swooping down to sidewalks for a meal and confidently strutting into people&#8217;s homes.</p>\n<p>Often nesting in park trees, the now-ubiquitous birds are known to scour fish markets at close to scavenge the day&#8217;s unsold product and visit the zoo at feeding time. They&#8217;ve integrated themselves so wholly into the lives of the city&#8217;s human inhabitants that it&#8217;s not uncommon for residents to supply food and respite to the striped creatures. &#8220;They have names for them, like Kiri the heron, who comes by every day for a snack and is not scared to enter the house,&#8221; the Prague-born photographer says. &#8220;At times he stays for a while and watches TV.&#8221;</p>\n<p>Last year, Hrudová released <a href=\"https://www.instagram.com/stories/highlights/17882556607600111/\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noopener\">a zine</a> compiling many of the images shown here, and she&#8217;s currently working on a new book titled <a href=\"https://www.voordekunst.nl/projecten/11387-chasing-amsterdam-julie-hrudova-1\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noopener\"><em>Chasing Amsterdam</em></a> that will be filled with the street photos she takes on a weekly basis. Densely assembled and often suspended from the ceiling, his recurring &#8220;<a href=\"https://www.ianberry.org/secret-garden\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noopener\">Secret Garden</a>&#8221; is comprised of blooms and leafy plants created entirely from recycled denim, producing immersive spaces teeming with indigo botanicals in various washes and fades.</p>\n<p>Since its debut at the <a href=\"https://cmany.org/\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noopener\">New York Children&#8217;s Museum of the Arts</a>, Berry&#8217;s site-specific installation has undergone a few iterations. &#8220;The first one was made with children in mind&#8230; hence the more magical secret garden angle,&#8221; he says, &#8220;just wanting to (ensure they think about) where the material comes from, see what they can make, and seek out outdoor places within a city.&#8221; It&#8217;s since traveled to London, Barcelona, The Netherlands, France, Kentucky, and the <a href=\"https://www.newsfflowermart.com\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noopener\">San Francisco Flower Mart</a>, where it&#8217;s permanently installed as <a href=\"https://www.ianberry.org/san-fran-flower-mart\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noopener\">a trellis</a> lining the space&#8217;s windows.</p>\n<p>&#160;</p>\n<div id=\"attachment_147246\" style=\"width: 1650px\" class=\"wp-caption alignnone\"><img aria-describedby=\"caption-attachment-147246\" loading=\"lazy\" class=\"wp-image-147246 size-full\" src=\"https://www.thisiscolossal.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/07/berry-3-scaled.jpg\" alt=\"\" width=\"1640\" height=\"2560\" srcset=\"https://www.thisiscolossal.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/07/berry-3-scaled.jpg 1640w, https://www.thisiscolossal.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/07/berry-3-640x999.jpg 640w, https://www.thisiscolossal.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/07/berry-3-960x1499.jpg 960w, https://www.thisiscolossal.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/07/berry-3-984x1536.jpg 984w, https://www.thisiscolossal.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/07/berry-3-1312x2048.jpg 1312w, https://www.thisiscolossal.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/07/berry-3-624x974.jpg 624w, https://www.thisiscolossal.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/07/berry-3-640x999@2x.jpg 1280w\" sizes=\"(max-width: 1640px) 100vw, 1640px\" /><p id=\"caption-attachment-147246\" class=\"wp-caption-text\">&#8220;Secret Garden,&#8221; Although much of Berry&#8217;s works recycle discarded jeans, jackets, and materials that are unusable for garments and employ environmentally conscious companies like <a href=\"https://www.tonello.com/en\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noopener\">Tonello</a> to wash and laser the vines, sustainability is an ancillary element of his practice.</p>\n<p>Instead, the East London-based artist focuses on generating a broader conversation about the ways communities change over time and a hope that people will find magic where it&#8217;s not necessarily expected.  &#8220;The piece was born out of the idea that in New York, many children would grow up without a garden, and as much of my work is about the community in urban environments,&#8221; he shares. &#8220;I wanted afterwards for the parents and children to go and seek them out—and they did.&#8221;</p>\n<p>&#8220;Secret Garden&#8221; is on view as part of Berry&#8217;s solo show <em>Splendid Isolation</em>, which is up through August 15 at <a href=\"https://museumrijswijk.nl/\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noopener\">Museum Rijswijk</a> in the Hague, The Netherlands. In October, his work is headed to the <a href=\"https://textilmuseet.se/\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noopener\">Textil Museet</a> in Sweden, where it&#8217;ll be until May 2022.
