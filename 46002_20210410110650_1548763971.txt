It’s such a great cake to have for festive celebrations – there are so many flavour combos you can do with the cake, filling and glaze, and so many ways to decorate it. I stick with the same roll cake recipe from my mum’s family cookbook and just adapt it differently each time.</p>\n\n\n\n<p>This year I’ve made things a bit fancier &#38; sophisticated by using Doves Farm Organic Wholemeal Rye Flour (available from Ocado, Sainsbury’s, Tesco and dovesfarm.co.uk) and a touch of cocoa in the sponge. The rye flour really helps to create the softest sponge and highlights the earthy nuttiness of the cocoa powder too. Doves Farm is the UK’s #1 organic flour<a> </a>brand (Kantar Worldpanel 52 w/e 21 April 2019) who produce a range of amazing flours, from traditional types like plain white and self-raising, to their ancient grains range which includes rye and spelt.</p>\n\n\n\n<figure class=\"wp-block-image size-full is-resized\"><img loading=\"lazy\" src=\"https://topwithcinnamon.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/12/Chocolate-Rye-Amaretto-Roll-Cake59.jpg\" alt=\"\" class=\"wp-image-19642\" width=\"650\" height=\"813\" srcset=\"https://topwithcinnamon.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/12/Chocolate-Rye-Amaretto-Roll-Cake59.jpg 1300w, https://topwithcinnamon.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/12/Chocolate-Rye-Amaretto-Roll-Cake59-240x300.jpg 240w, https://topwithcinnamon.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/12/Chocolate-Rye-Amaretto-Roll-Cake59-650x813.jpg 650w, https://topwithcinnamon.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/12/Chocolate-Rye-Amaretto-Roll-Cake59-768x960.jpg 768w, https://topwithcinnamon.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/12/Chocolate-Rye-Amaretto-Roll-Cake59-1229x1536.jpg 1229w, https://topwithcinnamon.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/12/Chocolate-Rye-Amaretto-Roll-Cake59-1080x1350.jpg 1080w, https://topwithcinnamon.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/12/Chocolate-Rye-Amaretto-Roll-Cake59-600x750.jpg 600w\" sizes=\"(max-width: 650px) 100vw, 650px\" /></figure>\n\n\n\n<p>To add extra layers of chocolatey-ness I included melted dark chocolate in the whipped cream filling which is also spiked with Amaretto for that boozy hit. If you’re serving it to kids though, feel free to replace the alcohol with some brewed, cooled coffee with a touch of almond extract.</p>\n\n\n\n<p>The most satisfying part about making this cake is the glaze – just a simple milk chocolate ganache – which you get to pour over the cake, enrobing it in a shiny, glistening coat. I decorated this with flaked almonds, some snowflake sprinkles and edible gold dust but a simpler option is to fork through the ganache (once set) in long streaks to give it a ‘tree bark’ texture and then dust lightly with icing sugar ‘snow’.
