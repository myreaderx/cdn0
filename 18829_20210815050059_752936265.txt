For those unaware, <strong>Act of Denial</strong> are a modern melodeath supergroup consisting of <strong>Soilwork</strong> singer Bjorn “Speed” Strid, <strong>Testament</strong>/ex-<strong>Death</strong> bassist Steve Di Giorgio, <strong>Night</strong> <strong>Flight</strong> <strong>Orchestra</strong> keyboardist John Lönnmyr, ex-<strong>Koziac</strong> (me neither…) guitarists Nikola Bartulić and Vojan Koceić, and <strong>Septicflesh</strong>/ex-<strong>Decapitated</strong>/living monolith Kerim “Krimh” Lechner behind the kit.</p>\n\n\n\n<p>Their sound is pretty similar to what Soilwork have been doing over the past decade or so, but with an added sense of heaviness and aggression that positions it closer to <em>The Ride Majestic</em> and<em> The Panic Broadcast </em>than <em>Verkligheten</em> or <em>NFO</em>. Strid is stellar as always, and Krimh and Di Giorgio definitely make their presence felt, but it&#8217;s the relative unknown Bartulić and Koceić who steal the show, consistently dishing out colossal riffs and leads like it’s no big deal.
