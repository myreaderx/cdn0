Comprised of numerous pieces cut from metal sheets, the materials lend themselves to organic forms like feathers, scales, wings, or the armaments of crustaceans. Seccull&#8217;s work scales up dramatically in her <a href=\"http://georgieseccull.com/art-installations\" rel=\"noopener noreferrer\" target=\"_blank\">installation practice</a> where she&#8217;s filled entire rooms and atriums with suspended pieces.  </p>\n<p>“We are born out of chaos in darkness and come into the light—my process is much the same: I begin with a thousand pieces scattered on the ground, then working almost like a jigsaw puzzle, I pick them up one by one and allow each piece to come together organically and dictate the outcome,” the artist shares in a statement. </p>\n<p>One of Seccull&#8217;s most recent sculptures has been nominated for a <a href=\"https://beautifulbizarreartprize.art/contest-image/6385/?fbclid=IwAR0BHaPajB42w6uO_9DZ3pJSIA8Mudr9ELQWjXL4__21g3QBMpsN9XiI48c\" rel=\"noopener noreferrer\" target=\"_blank\">Beautiful Bizarre</a> People&#8217;s Choice art prize, and she has an upcoming solo show at the <a href=\"https://www.gasworks.org.au/\" rel=\"noopener noreferrer\" target=\"_blank\">Gasworks Art Park</a> near Melbourne. Shared with permisison.</p>\n</div> \n<p>Bowerbirds are renowned for one of the most unusual courtship behaviors in the animal kingdom, where males build <a href=\"https://www.pinterest.com/PixieCurio/bowerbird-nests/\" rel=\"noopener noreferrer\" target=\"_blank\">elaborately decorated</a> nests—called bowers—in an attempt to court a mate. Kosovar visual artist <a href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Petrit_Halilaj\" rel=\"noopener noreferrer\" target=\"_blank\">Petrit Halilaj</a> drew inspiration from this unique ritual for his first solo exhibition at <a href=\"https://www.museoreinasofia.es/\" rel=\"noopener noreferrer\" target=\"_blank\">Reina Sofia’s Palacio de Cristal</a> (<a href=\"https://www.thisiscolossal.com/2014/01/a-reflective-palace-of-rainbows-by-kimsooja/\" rel=\"noopener noreferrer\" target=\"_blank\">previously</a>) in Madrid. Titled “<em>To a raven and the hurricanes which bring back smells of humans in love from unknown places</em>,” the installation serves as a metaphorical nest that connects the inside and outside spaces of the palace and features several avian elements like trays of birdseed and a giant pair of bird’s feet that descend from above. </p> \n<p>The collection of artworks is actually a collaborative effort between Halijaj and his life partner artist <a href=\"http://www.alvarourbano.com/\" rel=\"noopener noreferrer\" target=\"_blank\">Álvaro Urbano</a>, who helped construct the oversized forsythia, palm seeds, cherry blossom, poppy, carnation, and lily that fill the space. “I wanted to conceive Palacio de Cristal as a place for the celebration of love,” Halijaj shares. From the museum’s release:</p> \n<blockquote> \n <p>There is something strange and disproportionate about the size of this nest, the gigantic scale of its flowers, and the comfort and centrality it offers the birds. The artist thus suspends the logo-centric perspective that makes us believe we are the center and measure of all things, encouraging us to recognize ourselves as just one more element among many. The nest is thus revealed as the setting for a ritual that lies in wait for encounters, alliances and unions among its different visitors, altering and changing with the space.</p> \n</blockquote>
