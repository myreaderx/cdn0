I’m talking about leveraging recent data, leading indicators of your customers and their interest and propensity to travel with you.&nbsp;</span></span></span></span></span></p> \n<p class=\"Body\" style=\"border:none\"><span style=\"font-size:12pt\"><span style=\"font-family:Calibri,sans-serif\"><span style=\"color:black\"><span style=\"font-size:11.0pt\"><span style=\"font-family:&quot;Oracle Sans&quot;,sans-serif\">Recent </span></span></span><u><strong><span style=\"font-family:&quot;Oracle Sans&quot;,sans-serif\"><a href=\"https://blogs.oracle.com/marketingcloud/understanding-marketing-metrics%253a-email-open-rates\" target=\"_blank\"><span style=\"color:#2980b9;\"><span class=\"Hyperlink0\" style=\"font-family:Arial,sans-serif\"><span style=\"text-decoration:underline\"><span style=\"font-size:11.0pt\"><span style=\"font-family:&quot;Oracle Sans&quot;,sans-serif\">email open</span></span></span></span></span></a></span></strong></u><span style=\"color:black\"><span style=\"font-size:11.0pt\"><span style=\"font-family:&quot;Oracle Sans&quot;,sans-serif\"> and </span></span></span><span style=\"font-size:11.0pt\"><span style=\"font-family:&quot;Oracle Sans&quot;,sans-serif\"><u><strong><a href=\"http://blogs.oracle.com/marketingcloud/understanding-marketing-metrics-email-click-rates\" target=\"_blank\"><span style=\"color:#2980b9;\">click data</span></a></strong></u></span></span><span style=\"color:black\"><span style=\"font-size:11.0pt\"><span style=\"font-family:&quot;Oracle Sans&quot;,sans-serif\">—especially click data that’s tied to specific content categories—is highly instructive regarding intent. Same with site visit data from customers that are logged in. Connect that with frequency of engagement, and you get a sense of which customers have an increasing engagement velocity, which indicates a rising interest and openness to travel. The same holds true with your push and SMS messaging and so on.</span></span></span></span></span></p> \n<p class=\"Body\" style=\"border:none\"><span style=\"font-size:12pt\"><span style=\"font-family:Calibri,sans-serif\"><span style=\"color:black\"><span style=\"font-size:11.0pt\"><span style=\"font-family:&quot;Oracle Sans&quot;,sans-serif\">Make sure you’re using all of the data trails at your disposal to optimize the experience for your customers, because different travelers will be ready at different times. Smart brands are ready when their customers are.&nbsp;</span></span></span></span></span></p> \n<p class=\"Body\" style=\"border:none\"><span style=\"font-size:12pt\"><span style=\"font-family:Calibri,sans-serif\"><span style=\"color:black\"><span style=\"font-size:11.0pt\"><span style=\"font-family:&quot;Oracle Sans&quot;,sans-serif\"><strong>4</strong>.&nbsp;</span></span><b><span style=\"font-size:11.0pt\"><span style=\"font-family:&quot;Oracle Sans&quot;,sans-serif\">Communicate</span></span></b></span></span></span></p> \n<p class=\"Body\" style=\"border:none\"><span style=\"font-size:12pt\"><span style=\"font-family:Calibri,sans-serif\"><span style=\"color:black\"><span style=\"font-size:11.0pt\"><span style=\"font-family:&quot;Oracle Sans&quot;,sans-serif\">Using whatever customer touchpoints available, tell your customers what they need to know to ensure their experience is as good as possible. Tell them what you’re doing to make their visit safe and enjoyable—even better, show them via videos. Explain to them how their contactless experience will work from beginning to end. Highlight your cleaning protocols and staff training. Communication is important.</span></span></span></span></span></p> \n<p class=\"Body\" style=\"border:none\"><span style=\"font-size:12pt\"><span style=\"font-family:Calibri,sans-serif\"><span style=\"color:black\"><span style=\"font-size:11.0pt\"><span style=\"font-family:&quot;Oracle Sans&quot;,sans-serif\">Note that some of this messaging is sensitive and may not make for the most comfortable content. For example, some travelers aren’t nervous about the measures hotels or airlines are taking, but rather how their fellow travelers will react and whether they’ll comply. In the airline industry alone, </span></span></span><u><strong><span style=\"font-family:&quot;Oracle Sans&quot;,sans-serif\"><a href=\"https://nypost.com/2020/09/07/more-than-700-airline-passengers-banned-for-refusing-to-wear-masks/\" target=\"_blank\"><span style=\"color:#2980b9;\"><span class=\"Hyperlink1\" style=\"font-family:Arial,sans-serif\"><span style=\"text-decoration:underline\"><span style=\"font-size:11.0pt\"><span style=\"font-family:&quot;Oracle Sans&quot;,sans-serif\">hundreds of passengers have been banned by airlines</span></span></span></span></span></a></span></strong></u><span style=\"color:black\"><span style=\"font-size:11.0pt\"><span style=\"font-family:&quot;Oracle Sans&quot;,sans-serif\"> because of incidents that have led to flight delays and cancellations.</span></span></span></span></span></p> \n<p class=\"Body\" style=\"border:none\"><span style=\"font-size:12pt\"><span style=\"font-family:Calibri,sans-serif\"><span style=\"color:black\"><span style=\"font-size:11.0pt\"><span style=\"font-family:&quot;Oracle Sans&quot;,sans-serif\">Simply telling your customers what your expectations are for their behavior may not be enough. The industry might consider having travelers agree to observe all safety measures as a part of their reservation, with financial and other penalties spelled out should they break the agreement. It may also be time for airlines to share their no-fly lists with one another so that bad actors can’t simply take their dangerous behavior to another carrier. These are difficult discussions, but may be necessary to overcome the hesitations of cautious travelers.</span></span></span></span></span></p> \n<p class=\"Body\" style=\"border:none\"><span style=\"font-size:12pt\"><span style=\"font-family:Calibri,sans-serif\"><span style=\"color:black\"><span style=\"font-size:11.0pt\"><span style=\"font-family:&quot;Oracle Sans&quot;,sans-serif\">Finally, </span></span><span style=\"font-size:11.0pt\"><span style=\"font-family:&quot;Oracle Sans&quot;,sans-serif\">put your social media and PR teams on high alert and prepared to manage issues as people get back into traveling. Images of people violating established protocols are some of the most prone to be shared right now, so preparedness on this front is an absolute requirement.</span></span></span></span></span></p> \n<p class=\"Body\" style=\"border:none\"><span style=\"font-size:12pt\"><span style=\"font-family:Calibri,sans-serif\"><span style=\"color:black\"><b><span style=\"font-size:11.0pt\"><span style=\"font-family:&quot;Oracle Sans&quot;,sans-serif\">5. Validate that what used to work, still does</span></span></b></span></span></span></p> \n<p class=\"Body\" style=\"border:none\"><span style=\"font-size:12pt\"><span style=\"font-family:Calibri,sans-serif\"><span style=\"color:black\"><span style=\"font-size:11.0pt\"><span style=\"font-family:&quot;Oracle Sans&quot;,sans-serif\">What worked in early 2020 may not work now. </span></span></span><u><strong><span style=\"font-family:&quot;Oracle Sans&quot;,sans-serif\"><a href=\"https://blogs.oracle.com/marketingcloud/ab-testing-pitfalls%253a-how-marketers-can-avoid-costly-mistakes\" target=\"_blank\"><span style=\"color:#2980b9;\"><span class=\"Hyperlink1\" style=\"font-family:Arial,sans-serif\"><span style=\"text-decoration:underline\"><span style=\"font-size:11.0pt\"><span style=\"font-family:&quot;Oracle Sans&quot;,sans-serif\">A/B testing</span></span></span></span></span></a></span></strong></u><span style=\"color:black\"><span style=\"font-size:11.0pt\"><span style=\"font-family:&quot;Oracle Sans&quot;,sans-serif\"> is low-cost,&nbsp;</span></span><span style=\"font-size:11.0pt\"><span style=\"font-family:&quot;Oracle Sans&quot;,sans-serif\">high-reward method of ensuring that your messaging is aligned with your customers’ needs. Spend some cycles testing imagery, content, and timing for all your digital marketing and communication channels.&nbsp;</span></span></span></span></span></p> \n<p class=\"Body\" style=\"border:none\"><span style=\"font-size:12pt\"><span style=\"font-family:Calibri,sans-serif\"><span style=\"color:black\"><span style=\"font-size:11.0pt\"><span style=\"font-family:&quot;Oracle Sans&quot;,sans-serif\">The same holds true for your website, landing pages, and mobile app. Identify your most valuable touchpoints in your engagement ecosystem to ensure they reflect what travelers value today through validation and optimization testing. This would most certainly include your booking process, but may also include your cancellation process, rebooking process, application process for your private label credit card, and other touchpoints.</span></span></span></span></span></p> \n<p class=\"Body\" style=\"border:none\"><span style=\"font-size:12pt\"><span style=\"font-family:Calibri,sans-serif\"><span style=\"color:black\"><b><span style=\"font-size:11.0pt\"><span style=\"font-family:&quot;Oracle Sans&quot;,sans-serif\">6. Add “</span></span></b><b><span lang=\"NL\" style=\"font-size:11.0pt\"><span style=\"font-family:&quot;Oracle Sans&quot;,sans-serif\">snooze</span></span></b><b><span style=\"font-size:11.0pt\"><span style=\"font-family:&quot;Oracle Sans&quot;,sans-serif\">” functionality to ward off brand disengagers</span></span></b></span></span></span></p> \n<p class=\"Body\" style=\"border:none\"><span style=\"font-size:12pt\"><span style=\"font-family:Calibri,sans-serif\"><span style=\"color:black\"><span style=\"font-size:11.0pt\"><span style=\"font-family:&quot;Oracle Sans&quot;,sans-serif\">Right now, anyone who unsubscribes from your email, SMS, and push messages may simply be doing so because they know they won’t be traveling for many months and therefore don’t want any messages from you.
