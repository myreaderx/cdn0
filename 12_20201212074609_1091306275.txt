Organic ephemera—human hair, thorned branches, scavenged wings—become poignant installations and smaller artworks that ruminate on a myriad of global issues, including the climate crisis and the infinite failures of the medical establishment.</p>\n<p>Aparicio shares that her explorations of life and death began during childhood when she watched the natural world cycle through growth and decay in the woods outside of Barcelona. This lasting fascination has crystallized in the artist&#8217;s body of work, particularly in pieces like &#8220;Velo de luto (Mourning veil),&#8221; which sews together 1,365 seventeen-year cicada wings with strands of hair taken from two generations of women. The shrouds typically are worn to honor a spouse who&#8217;s died, and Aparicio notes the material and form exemplify that &#8220;as the fragility of the veil of wings decay so does the patriarchal veil of history that it represents.&#8221;</p>\n<p>&#160;</p>\n<div id=\"attachment_138698\" style=\"width: 1522px\" class=\"wp-caption alignnone\"><img aria-describedby=\"caption-attachment-138698\" loading=\"lazy\" class=\"wp-image-138698 size-full\" src=\"https://www.thisiscolossal.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/12/aparicio-11.jpg\" alt=\"\" width=\"1512\" height=\"1726\" srcset=\"https://www.thisiscolossal.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/12/aparicio-11.jpg 1512w, https://www.thisiscolossal.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/12/aparicio-11-640x731.jpg 640w, https://www.thisiscolossal.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/12/aparicio-11-960x1096.jpg 960w, https://www.thisiscolossal.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/12/aparicio-11-1346x1536.jpg 1346w, https://www.thisiscolossal.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/12/aparicio-11-624x712.jpg 624w, https://www.thisiscolossal.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/12/aparicio-11-640x731@2x.jpg 1280w\" sizes=\"(max-width: 1512px) 100vw, 1512px\" /><p id=\"caption-attachment-138698\" class=\"wp-caption-text\">&#8220;Childhood Memories&#8221; (2017), hand-carved rug into utility oak wood floor, 657 square-feet. Commenting broadly on the unjust power dynamics inherent within traditional healthcare, the artwork draws a direct correlation between the invasive and painful processes of medicine for women and their ability to bring new life into the world.</p>\n<p>Although she spends half her time in Barcelona, Aparicio is currently in Chicago and has work on view at two locations: her piece &#8220;Hopscotch&#8221; is part of <a href=\"https://mcachicago.org/\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noopener noreferrer\">MCA</a>’s group exhibition <em><a href=\"https://mcachicago.org/Exhibitions/2020/The-Long-Dream\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noopener noreferrer\">The Long Dream</a></em>, while her solo show <em>Hysteria</em> is at the <a href=\"https://imss.org/?mc_cid=5f0949833b&#38;mc_eid=[UNIQID]\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noopener noreferrer\" data-saferedirecturl=\"https://www.google.com/url?q=https://imss.org/?mc_cid%3D5f0949833b%26mc_eid%3D%5BUNIQID%5D&#38;source=gmail&#38;ust=1607788555274000&#38;usg=AFQjCNFcLBpm1mCTnd-KYGskLqe7syQweg\">International Museum of Surgical Science</a>, where the artist is in residence.
