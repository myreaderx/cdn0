The design studio <a href=\"https://100architects.com/\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noopener noreferrer\">100 Architects</a> installs bold interventions that transform typical urban spaces into colorful playgrounds. Each public structure, which boasts entertainment for both kids and adults, is conceptualized around a theme, whether a massive shower complete with ground-level waves or an illuminated castle with inner tunnels. Splashes of color, playground equipment like swings and slides, and towering structures frame the areas, while some spaces, like the &#8220;Sea Horse,&#8221; sprinkle water from a central spot.</p>\n<p>100 Architects recently partnered with urban planning group <a href=\"https://www.playgones.com/\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noopener noreferrer\">Playgones</a> to collaborate on a range of interventions throughout Europe in upcoming months, which you can follow on <a href=\"https://www.instagram.com/100architects/?hl=en\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noopener noreferrer\">Instagram</a>. target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noopener noreferrer\">Yellena James</a> cultivates brightly-hued ecosystems ripe with lines, patterns, and nature-based motifs. The Portland-based artist paints organic forms that resemble both marine species like coral and kelp in addition to full-bloom flowers, creating brilliant, labyrinth-like ecosystems. Although <a href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prussian_blue#:~:text=In%20medicine%2C%20orally%20administered%20Prussian,and%20radioactive%20isotopes%20of%20caesium.&#38;text=Prussian%20blue%20lent%20its%20name,(%22blue%20acid%22).\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noopener noreferrer\">Prussian blue</a> ink has been a mainstay in James&#8217;s practice for years, she recently discovered that the specific color serves as a remedy for certain toxic metal poisonings. This realization spurred the series shown here, which is aptly named <em>Antidote</em>. Each work features the vibrant hue in some capacity.</p>\n<p>If you&#8217;re in Portland, check out James&#8217;s solo show at <a href=\"http://www.stephaniechefas.com/\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noopener noreferrer\">Stephanie Chefas Projects</a> through October 10. target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noopener noreferrer\">Bouke de Vries</a> (<a href=\"https://www.thisiscolossal.com/?s=Bouke+de+Vries\">previously</a>) refers to some of his porcelain sculptures as &#8220;three-dimensional still lifes.&#8221; The artist, who was born in the Netherlands and now lives in London, creates sprawling assemblages that resemble a classic bowl of fruit or table setting frequently found in Dutch art. &#8220;I compose these pieces as, after the painter has finished with them, the ceramics get broken and decayed, and I breathe new life into them. The butterfly in still life is a symbol for the resurrection in (the) way I see what I do through my work,&#8221; he tells Colossal. In de Vries&#8217;s works, though, the seemingly mundane scenes are fractured with bursting ceramics, encroaching insects, and decaying fruit.</p>\n<p>The artist began working with porcelain as a restorer for 15 years before embarking on his own practice, which begins with a search for broken pottery and glass shards. He never breaks an undamaged piece but rather revitalizes those that are damaged already by creating new figures that celebrate the beauty of their previous forms. With a penchant for <a href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kintsugi\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noopener noreferrer\">Kintsugi</a>, he often utilizes gold lacquer to highlight the repaired cracks.</p>\n<p>Alongside sculptural still lifes, the figure of <a href=\"https://www.nationsonline.org/oneworld/Chinese_Customs/Guan_Yin.htm#:~:text=Chinese%20Bodhisattva%2F%20Goddess%20of%20Compassion,goddess%20and%20patron%20of%20seamen.&#38;text=The%20name%20Guan%20Yin%20also,the%20(human)%20World%22.\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noopener noreferrer\">Guan Yin</a>, the Buddhist goddess of compassion, mercy, and kindness, recurs in de Vries&#8217;s work. Often surrounded by cracked shards and recomposed garments, she conveys an ability for understanding and repair.</p>\n<p>In recent weeks, de Vries has been working on commissions and new pieces, in addition to a large-scale project that spans the entrance of one of Sotheby&#8217;s <a href=\"https://www.sothebys.com/en/series/showroom-the-london-edit?locale=en\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noopener noreferrer\">Bond Street galleries</a>, which you can see on <a href=\"https://www.instagram.com/p/CFuRNlKD_ki/\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noopener noreferrer\">Instagram</a>.
