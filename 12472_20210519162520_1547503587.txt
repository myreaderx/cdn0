The highly contested area was&nbsp;occupied by Israel in the 1967 Six-Day War, along with Gaza and other territory in the West Bank. Following Sharon’s visit, Palestinian protests sparked a violent crackdown by Israeli riot police, marking the beginning of five years of resistance&nbsp;known as the second intifada. The resulting fighting claimed thousands of lives, mostly Palestinian,&nbsp;and saw the rise of Hamas as a political force in Gaza.</p> \n   <p>Two decades later, history has repeated itself with Israeli aggression in East Jerusalem. Once again the al-Aqsa compound and Sharon’s party—headed since 2009 by Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu—are <a href=\"https://www.nytimes.com/2021/05/15/world/middleeast/israel-palestinian-gaza-war.html\" target=\"_blank\">at the center</a> of a wave of violence, following an Israeli police&nbsp;action at&nbsp;the al-Aqsa mosque on the first day of Ramadan&nbsp;and ongoing efforts to evict Palestinian families in the&nbsp;East Jerusalem neighborhood of Sheikh Jarrah. Now in its second week, the fighting has&nbsp;displaced tens of thousands amidst highly destructive Israeli airstrikes in Gaza.&nbsp;Over 200 people have died—again mostly Palestinian, including scores of children.</p> \n   <p>To shed badly needed context on the current crisis, we compiled some of our most searching essays on&nbsp;Israel’s occupation of Palestine. Contrary to Israeli claims of self-defense in response to acts of terrorism, these crises reflect decades of brutal Israeli aggression in Gaza and the West Bank.&nbsp;Above all, these pieces&nbsp;speak with moral clarity&nbsp;about&nbsp;the extreme imbalance of power between Israel and occupied Palestinian territories, the ongoing&nbsp;humanitarian&nbsp;crisis under Israel’s <a href=\"http://bostonreview.net/world/how-not-talk-about-gaza\">domination of Gaza</a>, and the far-right&nbsp;<a href=\"https://bostonreview.net/world/assaf-sharon-israel-idf-gaza-radicalism-moral-siege-kahane\">militarization of&nbsp;Jewish supremacism</a>&nbsp;in Israeli politics. Reflecting on his experiences as a solider in the Israeli army,&nbsp;philosopher Oded Na’aman <a href=\"http://bostonreview.net/world/checkpoint-oded-naaman\">disputes</a> official Israeli claims that an ethical occupation is possible, while the late Rabbi Ben-Zion Gold discusses <a href=\"https://bostonreview.net/world-us/ben-zion-gold-diaspora-and-intifada\">the responsibility of American Jews</a>&nbsp;to hold Israel accountable.</p> \n   <p>Several essays stress the primacy of politics alongside moral exhortation.&nbsp;A forum with Palestinian legal scholar Lama Abu-Odeh makes <a href=\"http://bostonreview.net/forum/lama-abu-odeh-case-binationalism\">the case for binationalism</a>, while other pieces&nbsp;scrutinize the <a href=\"http://bostonreview.net/books-ideas/assaf-sharon-israel-palestine-two-state\">limitations of a one-state solution</a>,&nbsp;the <a href=\"https://bostonreview.net/avner-inbar-assaf-sharon-sari-nusseibeh-palestinian-israeli-politics\">relationship between civil and political rights</a>, and <a href=\"http://bostonreview.net/politics/jeremy-pressman-two-state-head-one-state-heart\">enduring obstacles to&nbsp;two states</a>. Other contributors&nbsp;explore the uses and abuses of historical understanding, emphasizing how it is both <a href=\"https://bostonreview.net/joseph-levine-history-matters-palestine-israel\">essential for peace</a> and <a href=\"https://bostonreview.net/jeremy-pressman-lost-opportunities-israel-palestine\">liable to distortion</a>. In two feature reports from the late 2000s, Middle East correspondent Helena Cobban tracks in great detail how <a href=\"https://bostonreview.net/cobban-breakout-hamas-end-of-two-state-solution\">the rise of Hamas</a>&nbsp;and <a href=\"https://bostonreview.net/world/peace-out-helena-cobban\">the decline of the Israeli peace movement</a> transformed Israeli-Palestinian diplomacy. A final set of essays mines other forceful visions of Israeli-Palestinian life, from <a href=\"https://bostonreview.net/elias-koury-the-mirror-imagining-justice-in-palestine\">Israeli and Palestinian novels</a> and the <a href=\"http://bostonreview.net/philosophy-religion/esmat-elhalaby-world-edward-said\">work of Edward Said</a> to&nbsp;the largely forgotten <a href=\"https://bostonreview.net/politics/ezra-glinter-lost-utopias\">Jewish territorialist movement</a>, which imagined forms of Jewish self-determination beyond statehood in Palestine.</p> \n   <p>Together they offer an indispensable guide to the current crisis and the&nbsp;movement&nbsp;for&nbsp;peace.</p> \n   <hr /> \n   <p>&nbsp;<a href=\"https://bostonreview.net/world/how-not-talk-about-gaza\"><img alt=\"\" src=\"http://bostonreview.net/sites/default/files/1024px-Damaged_housing_gaza_strip_april_2009_1.jpg\" style=\"width: 740px; height: 500px;\" /></a></p> \n   <p><a href=\"https://bostonreview.net/world/how-not-talk-about-gaza\" target=\"_blank\">How Not to Talk About Gaza</a><br /> by <strong>Colin Dayan</strong></p> \n   <p>In the name of “security,” Israel has implemented something like a permanent state of emergency in the occupied territories of Gaza and the West Bank, where for decades brute force has coexisted with systematic discrimination, surveillance, and disappearance.</p> \n   <p>• • •</p> \n   <p><a href=\"https://bostonreview.net/world/oded-naaman-choosing-violence\"><img alt=\"\" src=\"http://bostonreview.net/sites/default/files/IDF%20Hebron%203%20Feature_0.jpg\" style=\"width: 740px; height: 500px;\" /></a></p> \n   <p><a href=\"https://bostonreview.net/world/oded-naaman-choosing-violence\" target=\"_blank\">Choosing Violence</a><br /> by&nbsp;<strong>Oded Na’aman</strong></p> \n   <p>As a child brought up in the Jewish-Israeli educational system, I was taught that none of Israel’s wars was a war of choice. Today I no longer believe this is true.</p> \n   <p>• • •</p> \n   <p><a href=\"https://bostonreview.net/joseph-levine-history-matters-palestine-israel\"><img alt=\"\" src=\"http://bostonreview.net/sites/default/files/gaza-3829405_960_720_0.jpg\" style=\"width: 740px; height: 500px;\" /></a></p> \n   <p><a href=\"https://bostonreview.net/joseph-levine-history-matters-palestine-israel\" target=\"_blank\">History Matters</a><br /> by <strong>Joseph Levine</strong></p> \n   <p>Without a proper appreciation of how we got here, the task at hand—forging a durable peace—will be extremely difficult, perhaps impossible to achieve. We need to begin from the fact that what the Zionists did in the name of the Jewish people was a serious injustice against the Palestinians.</p> \n   <p>• • •</p> \n   <p>&nbsp;<a href=\"https://bostonreview.net/books-ideas/assaf-sharon-israel-palestine-two-state\"><img alt=\"\" src=\"http://bostonreview.net/sites/default/files/Sharon---Ziodave-feature_0.jpg\" style=\"width: 740px;
