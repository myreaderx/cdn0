In October, R.J. Reynolds’ Vuse Solo became the first e-cigarette to clear the FDA’s process.</span></p><p style=\"text-align: left;line-height: 2em;\"><br></p><p style=\"text-align: left;line-height: 2em;\"><span style=\"font-family: Cambria, serif;letter-spacing: 0.544px;text-align: left;background-color: rgb(255, 255, 255);\">The FDA has so far denied or refused to review applications for more than 5 million vaping products. Now, though, that loophole is closing, meaning many products cannot continue to be sold.</span></p><p style=\"text-align: left;line-height: 2em;\"><br></p><p style=\"text-align: left;line-height: 2em;\"><span style=\"font-family: Cambria, serif;letter-spacing: 0.544px;text-align: left;background-color: rgb(255, 255, 255);\">In addition to the smaller companies likely to be affected by the change, Puff Bar, a popular brand of disposable e-cigarette, could be the biggest casualty. In a previous statement, a Juul spokesperson said that “illegally marketed and illicit products and products designed to evade federal and state oversight undermine harm reduction and a responsible e-vapor category.”</span></p><p style=\"text-align: left;line-height: 2em;\"><br></p><p style=\"text-align: left;line-height: 2em;\"><span style=\"font-family: Cambria, serif;letter-spacing: 0.544px;text-align: left;background-color: rgb(255, 255, 255);\">On that front, Juul and public-health groups are in rare agreement.
