The United States and other nations are expected to report a third-quarter surge too, but they are still behind or just catching up to pre-pandemic levels.</span></p><p style=\"text-align: left;\"><br></p><p style=\"text-align: left;\"><span style=\"color: rgb(62, 62, 62);font-family: Georgia, serif;font-size: 16px;letter-spacing: 0.54px;text-align: left;background-color: rgb(255, 255, 255);\">China’s lead could widen further in the months to come. It has almost no local transmission of the virus now, while the United States and Europe face another accelerating wave of cases.</span></p><p style=\"text-align: left;\"><br></p><p style=\"text-align: left;\"><span style=\"color: rgb(62, 62, 62);font-family: Georgia, serif;font-size: 16px;letter-spacing: 0.54px;text-align: left;background-color: rgb(255, 255, 255);\">The vigorous expansion of the Chinese economy means that it is set to dominate global growth — accounting for at least 30 percent of the world’s economic growth this year and in the years to come, Justin Lin Yifu, a cabinet adviser and honorary dean of the National School of Development at Peking University, said at a recent government news conference in Beijing.</span></p><p style=\"text-align: left;\"><br></p><p style=\"text-align: left;\"><span style=\"color: rgb(62, 62, 62);font-family: Georgia, serif;font-size: 16px;letter-spacing: 0.54px;text-align: left;background-color: rgb(255, 255, 255);\">Chinese companies are making up a greater share of the world’s exports, manufacturing consumer electronics, personal protection equipment and other goods in high demand during the pandemic.
