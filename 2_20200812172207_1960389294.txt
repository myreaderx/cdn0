More specifically, they wanted to see if they could use a vapor coating technique to turn ordinary red bricks into part of a <a href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Supercapacitor\">supercapacitor</a>. That actually isn’t <em>quite</em> as weird as it sounds, given that the red of a brick is an iron mineral, and iron is a common component of some battery chemistries. Bricks are often porous as well, meaning there is plenty of surface area where a thin coating could interact with that iron.</p>\n<p>The process (something they had developed previously) involves heating the brick in an enclosure along with hydrochloric acid and an organic compound that mercifully shortens to “EDOT.” The two liquid substances evaporate and condense on the brick’s convoluted surface.
