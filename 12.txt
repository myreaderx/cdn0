Few were what we would call &#8216;smart&#8217; toys, objects whose use could evolve together with our child,&#8221; Bistra says. This dissatisfaction inspired Anastas to utilize old floorboards to create the prototype for the minimal toys that the company offers today.</p>\n<p>Check out the video below to dive into ESNAF Toys&#8217; history and see more of its new dinosaur collection, which recently surpassed its fundraising goal on <a href=\"https://www.kickstarter.com/projects/esnaftoys/esnaf-toys-wooden-magnetic-toys-for-kids-and-adults?ref=user_menu\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noopener noreferrer\">Kickstarter</a>.
