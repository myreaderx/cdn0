Z4&#8217;s aesthetics, making one wonder how watered-down it&#8217;ll be when it reaches production. Although fairly futuristic in its design, like the Concept 8 Series, BMW insists both cars preview near-production-ready designs. We imagine things like wheel designs, side-view mirrors, and lighting details will change but overall, BMW promises the general design will remain.</p>\n<p>“The Concept Z4 expresses the new BMW design language from all perspectives and in all details,&#8221; said BMW&#8217;s chief designer, Adrian van Hooydonk. &#8220;From the dynamic-looking front to the striking flanks to the clean-cut tail end: a few lines and the subtle interplay between surfaces are enough to generate a sense of power and emotion.”</p>\n<h6><em><strong>What powers it?</strong></em></h6>\n<p>Not may details are available. Word on the street says it gets six-cylinder power, meaning it&#8217;s likely the latest and greatest rendition of BMW&#8217;s ubiquitous twin-turbo inline-six.</p>\n<p>Overall however, the Concept Z4 proves skeptical rumors wrong and breaks cover with a very eclectic, modern, and edgy design, thoroughly epitomizing a new modern peak in automotive design.</p>\n<p>I&#8217;m personally on the fence about it and would need to view it in person to get a better idea.
